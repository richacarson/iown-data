The Session

The tape divided cleanly along the fault line we have been watching all week. The S&P 500 closed –0.50% and the Nasdaq –1.45% as a second consecutive de-rating of the AI-hardware complex overwhelmed the broader market, while the Dow held modestly green. Beneath the index, the day was less a decline than a rotation — capital leaving semiconductors and moving into healthcare, staples and defensives.

The chip story set the tone before the open. Taiwan Semiconductor reported roughly 77% earnings growth and raised its 2026 capital-spending plan to $60–$64 billion, adding a further Arizona commitment — and still the shares gave back more than 4%. A dominant print that clears every estimate and is met with selling is the market telling you that expectations, not results, are the problem. The reaction rippled outward: SK Hynix fell about 11%, Korea’s KOSPI slid more than 6%, and memory names Sandisk and Western Digital led U.S. declines, with the semiconductor index off roughly 3–4%. The macro backdrop offered little offset — June retail sales rose an in-line 0.2%, jobless claims eased to 208,000 and regional manufacturing surveys firmed, but a fifth straight night of U.S. strikes on Iran kept oil and Treasury yields elevated.

The other side of the trade belonged to us. A wave of healthcare earnings redirected the flows: Abbott jumped to the top of the S&P leaderboard on a Q2 beat and a raised full-year outlook, and UnitedHealth’s decisive beat carried the Dow. The rotation split our two sleeves almost to the decimal it was designed to. The Dividend Strategy — anchored in healthcare, staples and financials and largely free of AI-hardware exposure — gained +1.19%, edging past a flat-to-lower SPY (–0.52%) while trailing a strong DVY (+1.70%). The Growth Portfolio’s semiconductor and digital-asset weighting absorbed the session’s pressure, closing –3.04% against IUSG’s –1.69%.

A day like this is the barbell working exactly as intended — one sleeve leaning into growth, the other into durable cash flows, with rotation moving the weight between them without breaking the frame. We own businesses, not the tape. A single session of profit-taking in names up triple digits for the year is the market catching its breath, not a thesis unwinding.

Holdings News

Abbott (ABT) — Q2 beat and raised guidance. Adjusted EPS of $1.31 topped the $1.28 consensus on revenue of $12.59 billion, up 13% reported. Management raised full-year adjusted EPS guidance to $5.45–$5.60 and reaffirmed comparable sales growth of 6.5–7.5%. Diagnostics rose 42.3% on a reported basis, lifted by the Exact Sciences acquisition and mid-teens Cologuard growth, while medical devices grew 9%. Management also flagged no expected downside from procedure-volume softness — a direct rebuttal to recent hospital-operator caution.

Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) — strong quarter, weak reaction. TSMC posted its fifth straight record quarter with roughly 77% earnings growth and lifted its 2026 capital-spending outlook, yet the shares closed –2.32% and set the tone for the sector. The pattern — a dominant print sold rather than bought — mirrors the earlier reaction to ASML and signals how demanding the bar has become for AI-linked names.
