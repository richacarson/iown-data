The Session

The chip flush that defined Tuesday did not extend. Semiconductors steadied across the board — NVDA –0.52%, AMD essentially flat, TSM +1.02%, ADI +1.45%, LRCX +0.93% — as the broad tape treaded water ahead of Micron’s after-the-bell report. The S&P 500 closed –0.10% at 7,358, the Nasdaq slipped 0.43%, and the Dow added 0.35% on its lighter technology weight and a sharp homebuilder rally.

Where the selling actually went was oil and the assets that key off it. Brent crude fell 4.3% to roughly $74 and WTI to about $70 — the lowest since early March — as tankers resumed transit through the Strait of Hormuz. That pulled the 10-year Treasury yield below 4.5%, and paired with a bipartisan affordable-housing bill clearing Congress, sent the PHLX Housing Index up 5.4%, with Toll Brothers gaining 6.67%. Energy holdings gave back with the crude move: CVX –2.57%, VLO –0.51%, DVN –1.50%.

The other pressure point was the dollar, which touched a 2026 high against a still-hawkish Fed backdrop. Non-yielding assets bore the brunt: gold fell 3.3% and bitcoin slid toward $60,000, dragging the digital sleeve (IBIT –4.08%, ETHA –4.79%) and the gold miners (AEM –4.18%). On the question the morning posed — whether the AI backbone would hold its post-flush levels — the answer was yes: Asia rebounded, with the Kospi recovering roughly 3%, and Wedbush’s channel checks reported no change in enterprise AI demand.

This is the rotation working as designed. The defensive tilt stayed intact, which is precisely why the cash-flow names held green — staples (CHD +2.27%, CL +0.49%), healthcare (DGX +3.01%, SYK +1.19%), and steady industrials (CAT +1.04%, FAST +1.45%) — while the Growth Portfolio gave back its commodity and digital exposure. The Dividend Strategy finished +0.32%, ahead of both DVY and the S&P; Growth eased –0.78%, a fraction of Tuesday’s move.

A rotation is not a regime change. Tuesday’s chip flush gave way to a crypto-and-commodity reset, and the durable cash-flow holdings absorbed it — which is exactly what owning quality is supposed to do. No framework trigger is in play; we stay the course and let the businesses compound.

Holdings News

Qualcomm (Dividend Strategy) confirmed a roughly $3.9 billion all-stock acquisition of AI-software startup Modular, announced on the same day as its investor day. Modular builds a vendor-neutral software layer that lets AI models run efficiently across chips from different makers — a direct push into the data-center software territory long anchored by Nvidia’s CUDA. The deal, led on Modular’s side by LLVM and Swift creator Chris Lattner, issues up to 19.2 million Qualcomm shares and is expected to close in the second half of 2026. QCOM still finished –3.29% as the broader chip caution and the all-stock dilution weighed on the session.

Micron reports after today’s close — the principal read-through for the semiconductor names across both sleeves (NVDA, AMD, TSM, ADI, LRCX, QCOM, NXPI, MRVL, KEYS, TEL). After Tuesday’s memory-led flush and Wednesday’s stabilization, the guide will set the tone for the AI-infrastructure complex into Thursday’s May PCE print.
