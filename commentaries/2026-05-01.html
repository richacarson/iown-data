The Session

The index made new highs, and our sleeves did not come along. The S&P 500 advanced 0.29% to a record 7,230.12 and the Nasdaq Composite jumped 0.89% to 25,114.44, also a record close, as Apple’s 3% post-earnings move and a continued AI-infrastructure bid carried mega-cap tech back to the front of the tape. The Dow slipped 0.31% to 49,499.27 with Chevron’s 1.39% decline and a soft industrial complex pulling the blue-chip average lower. The Russell 2000 added 0.46% to close at 2,812.82, just below its 52-week high.

Underneath the headline, the day’s mechanics were not friendly to our positioning. Yesterday’s broadening rally rotated capital into industrials and small-caps; today the rotation reversed, with leadership returning to the names we deliberately do not concentrate in. The ISM Manufacturing PMI held at 52.7 in line with March, but Prices Paid surged 6.3 points to 84.6, the highest reading since April 2022, and the Employment subindex slipped to 46.4 — the weakest of the year. The combination is a stagflationary print that will keep the rate-path debate live into the May 15 chair transition.

Crude pulled back as Iran reportedly responded to the latest U.S. ceasefire amendments through Pakistani mediators, though Brent held above $111 and the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed. The pullback hit the IOWN energy sleeve broadly — CVX –1.39%, OKE –2.27%, VLO –2.26%, CNX –2.65%. Bitcoin advanced 2.43% to $78,163, taking the digital-asset sleeve sharply higher. Gold eased 0.14% to $4,623; the VIX held at 16.98, a level that does not indicate stress despite the dispersion under the surface.

When the index makes a record print and the dividend sleeve trails by 112 basis points, the question is always — what did we own that the index did not? Today the answer is Stryker, where a single cyber-incident-driven Q1 miss accounted for the bulk of the relative drag. The earnings call confirmed full-year guidance and the M&A pipeline; the operational disruption is contained to one quarter. We hold.

Holdings News

SYK Q1 cyber-incident dispersion clarified. The Stryker earnings call last night confirmed an Iran-backed cyber attack disrupted operations from early March through early April, with the operational shutdown driving organic sales growth to just 2.4% for the quarter and adjusted EPS down 8.5% year-on-year. Adjusted gross margin contracted 190 basis points; operating margin compressed 180 basis points. Management held full-year guidance of 8.0–9.5% organic growth and adjusted EPS of $14.90–$15.10, citing ‘distortions that will normalize over the course of the year.’ CEO Lobo confirmed the M&A pipeline remains active with net debt to EBITDA at 2.1x. BTIG kept its Buy rating; consensus 12-month target sits at $420 versus today’s $294.73 close.

CVX Q1: massive EPS beat, mixed reception. Chevron reported adjusted EPS of $1.41 versus $0.97 consensus — the largest earnings beat since October 2020 — on revenue of $48.61B versus a higher $51.39B compiled estimate. Reported net income fell 36% to $2.2B due to roughly $2.9B in unfavorable timing effects on financial hedges and a $360M legal reserve. Adjusted free cash flow reached $4.1B; the company returned $6.0B to shareholders in the quarter ($3.5B dividends, $2.5B buybacks). Worldwide oil production rose 23.7% year-on-year on Hess integration and Permian/Gulf of America growth. Wirth: ‘Despite heightened geopolitical volatility... Chevron delivered solid first quarter performance.’ Stock closed –1.39% at $190.63 as the timing-effect noise overshadowed the headline beat.

CL Q1: clean acceleration, full-year guide held. Colgate-Palmolive reported Q1 net sales of $5.32B (+8.4% year-on-year), beating the $5.22B consensus, with adjusted EPS of $0.97 versus $0.95 expected. Organic sales growth accelerated to 3.3% from Q4’s 2.2%, with companywide volume growth of 1.1% and pricing contribution of 2.2%. North America volume declined 3.2% on trade-down behavior, offset by Asia-Pacific strength. Management maintained the FY26 organic sales growth range of 1–4% and full-year EPS guidance, while trimming gross margin guidance modestly. Stock closed +2.23% at $87.26.
