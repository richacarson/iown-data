The Session

The question the long weekend left open — whether last week’s chip give-back was a rotation or the start of something wider — got its answer within the first hour of the reopen, and the answer was rotation. The semiconductor complex that absorbed two sessions of concentrated selling before the holiday caught a broad, well-sponsored bid from the opening bell. The Nasdaq rose 1.13% to 26,125, the S&P 500 added 0.74% to close at 7,538.34, and the Dow gained 0.29% to finish above 53,000 for the first time. The VIX eased to 15.67.

The recovery had names attached to it. Goldman Sachs raised its AMD target to $640 from $450, explicitly treating last week’s pullback as an entry rather than a warning, and Cantor Fitzgerald went further to $700 — the highest target on the Street. Foxconn’s stronger-than-expected quarterly sales over the weekend gave the AI-demand thesis a fresh data point, Nvidia said its roadmap is intact after a server-delay report rattled Asia overnight, and Samsung’s Tuesday earnings now stand as the next proof point for the memory complex. The bid was not indiscriminate: homebuilders, retail, and parts of healthcare lagged as money moved back toward the cohort it had abandoned Wednesday and Thursday.

The macro tape cooperated quietly. The June ISM Services index printed 54.0 — a 24th straight month of expansion, with the employment component back above 50 for the first time in four months and the prices index at its lowest reading since February as fuel costs work their way back down. That combination — steady activity, firming services employment after Thursday’s soft payrolls print, cooling price pressure — is about as agreeable a first hard-data read as the reopen could have asked for ahead of Wednesday’s FOMC minutes, the first from a Warsh-chaired meeting. Oil kept drifting: OPEC+ approved a fifth consecutive monthly output increase for August on Sunday, roughly 188,000 barrels per day, and WTI held near $68 as the Hormuz premium continues to unwind. Energy was the day’s soft spot, and the funeral of Ayatollah Khamenei proceeded in Tehran with technical talks in Doha set to resume around July 11.

The digital sleeve extended its early-July repair. Bitcoin held near $63,500, spot Bitcoin ETFs have now snapped their long outflow streak, and both sleeve positions posted strong sessions alongside the crypto-adjacent equities.

No S&P 500 trim or buy thresholds are in play. The index sits near its highs, and the week’s rotation round-trip — out of chips Thursday, back in Monday — is exactly the kind of noise the framework is built to ignore. Both sleeves are well ahead of their benchmarks at the year’s midpoint.

Holdings News

Lockheed Martin confirmed its $3.5 billion acquisition of Ultra Maritime, the naval-defense specialist in anti-submarine sonar buoys and torpedo-detection systems, tracking toward roughly $784 million in 2026 revenue. The deal deepens the undersea franchise at a moment when maritime security commands a premium, and it is the company’s most significant corporate action of the year.

Qualcomm is reported to be in advanced talks to acquire Modular, an AI infrastructure software company, for roughly $4 billion — a software layer on top of the data-center silicon push that management outlined at its investor day, with fiscal 2027 data-center targets that analysts increasingly treat as supported by actual purchase commitments.

Analog Devices received a higher price target tied to accelerating AI chip demand, closing up 3.09% at $388.83, and Susquehanna lifted its target on Lam Research, which finished roughly flat after leading the pre-market. Vistra set its Q2 report for August 7.
