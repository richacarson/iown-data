The Session

The CPI print Monday’s record close set up arrived hot, and the tape paid the tax. Headline April CPI rose 0.6% month-over-month and 3.8% year-over-year, both above the 3.7% consensus, with energy contributing roughly 40% of the gain on gasoline up 28.4% annually. Core ticked to 2.8% YoY against 2.7% expected. The S&P 500 closed at 7,401.63 (–0.15%), the Nasdaq Composite at 26,090.30 (–0.70%), and the Russell 2000 at 2,842.84 (–0.97%); the Dow finished modestly higher at 49,760.55 (+0.11%) on the defensive bid. The composition told the story: large-cap value held, growth pulled back, and the inflation tax landed where it was always going to land first.

Semiconductors absorbed the bulk of the repricing. QCOM declined 11.46% to $210.31 after Monday’s 8.42% record close near $237.53, marking the largest single-day reversal in the name since 2020 per CNBC reporting. The sector context: a hot CPI argues against near-term rate cuts and re-rates high-duration growth equities; per Trump’s public commentary and Reuters reporting, the U.S.-Iran ceasefire remains under strain after the May 7 incident in which three U.S. destroyers were targeted in the Strait of Hormuz. Brent and WTI proxies in the holdings showed continued energy strength, though today’s rotation was muted relative to Monday’s 3.6% Brent move.

Under the surface, the Dividend Strategy did its job. Defensive medical (SYK +4.48%, ABT +2.17%, CHD +3.19%), industrials with backlog (TEL +3.69%, LMT +1.71%), and steady payroll (ADP +1.01%) carried the strategy through what was, on the Growth side, a more difficult tape. VIX closed at 17.94 (–2.39%), a notable detail — the move was a rotation, not a regime change. Gold eased to $4,717.30 (–0.24%) and bitcoin softened to $80,783 (–1.15%) as the dollar firmed on the hawkish CPI implication.

A hot inflation print that the VIX takes in stride at 17.94 is the market telling us this is repositioning, not panic. The Dividend Strategy’s outperformance against the Growth Portfolio today reaffirms the diversification thesis at the strategy level.
