The Session

The market staged a choppy advance into the close as the post-Nvidia tape clarified rather than confirmed the AI-infrastructure thesis. The S&P 500 added 0.18% to 7,446, the Dow led the headline indices at +0.55%, and the Nasdaq Composite eked out a 0.09% gain after spending much of the morning underwater. Small caps carried the day with the Russell 2000 up 0.93%, the VIX eased nearly 4% to 16.75, and the bond market stabilized after a volatile open.

Nvidia’s “measured response” setup resolved measured: shares closed down 1.77% to $219.51 despite Wednesday night’s record print — $81.6 billion in Q1 revenue, an $80 billion buyback, and a $91 billion Q2 guide. A clean beat across the board, met with a clean sell-the-news. The same script played out one notch louder in Analog Devices, which followed Wednesday’s 7%+ slide with another 3.48% decline to $384.21 even after a record-revenue print and a $1.5 billion Empower Semiconductor acquisition aimed squarely at AI power delivery. Beat the number, announce the deal, and watch the stock fade — the market priced both of these prints well before they crossed the tape.

The split inside the chip complex is the real story. Qualcomm jumped 5.38% to $213.41 on confirmation of an expanded multi-year technology collaboration with Stellantis, extending its automotive AI footprint. Lam Research added 3.48% to $302.24 on the same capital-intensity argument that lifted it Wednesday. Marvell tacked on another 2.08% and Credo Technology rallied 5.69% on the connectivity build-out. Against that, NXP Semiconductors gave back 3.47% and Nvidia itself eased on the print — the market is paying up for the next leg of the AI capex cycle while questioning the names already priced for it.

Iran’s Supreme Leader reportedly issued a directive to keep enriched uranium inside the country, complicating the de-escalation arc the prior session priced in. Oil whipsawed through the morning before settling: WTI and Brent both gave more ground, and the energy sleeve continued to absorb the unwind of the war-risk premium. Valero ex-dated its $1.20 dividend today and closed down 5.00%, with Devon Energy off 2.79% and CNX off 2.68%.

The sleeves are doing exactly what they were built to do. The AI-infrastructure thesis is broader than any single name, and today’s rotation from Nvidia toward Qualcomm, Lam, Marvell, and Credo — alongside the power and foundry positions — is the discipline working as designed.
