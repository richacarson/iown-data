The Session

Equities recovered most of the day’s losses as oil reversed sharply and Israel signaled it was helping the U.S. reopen the Strait of Hormuz. The S&P 500 fell 0.28% to 6,606, paring what had been a 1% drop at the morning low. The Dow lost 204 points (–0.44%) to 46,021. The Nasdaq slipped 0.28% to 22,091. The Russell 2000 was the session’s standout, finishing up 0.65% at 2,495 as small-cap energy and financial names outperformed.

Oil was the main event. Brent crude spiked above $119 intraday after Iranian strikes hit Qatari LNG infrastructure and a Saudi refinery, briefly removing an estimated 17% of global LNG production. The spike reversed hard once Prime Minister Netanyahu told media that Israel was actively assisting in reopening the Strait of Hormuz and that Iran could no longer enrich uranium or manufacture ballistic missiles. Brent settled near $108.65, up 1.2% on the day. WTI closed at $96.14, roughly flat.

The Fed held rates at 3.50–3.75% as expected, projecting one cut for the remainder of 2026. Chair Powell called growth “solid” and sidestepped direct comment on the conflict, though the FOMC statement acknowledged “elevated uncertainty.” Markets have now effectively priced out any near-term easing. Gold fell nearly 5% to $4,659 as the dollar strengthened on the hawkish hold, and Bitcoin slipped 1.3% to $70,322.

The S&P 500 is trading near its 200-day moving average around 6,600. Drawdown from the January high of 7,002 sits at roughly –5.7%. Our BUY tranches begin at –20% from the all-time high—well below current levels. Patience is the position.
