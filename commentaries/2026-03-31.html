The Session

The hope trade that opened and closed in a single session on Monday held all day Tuesday. Reports that President Trump told aides he would consider ending the Iran war—even without the Strait of Hormuz fully reopened—ignited a broad rally that gained momentum into the close. The S&P 500 surged 2.92% to 6,528.65, its strongest session since May. The Nasdaq led at +3.83%, the Dow added 1,025 points (+2.49%), and the Russell 2000 gained 3.41%. Of 500 constituents, 441 finished higher. Ten of eleven sectors advanced; energy was the sole decliner as WTI pulled back to $101.38, down 1.5% on the session.

The VIX collapsed 17.2% to 25.35—still elevated, but a meaningful retreat from the 31+ levels seen last Friday. Treasuries extended their advance as yields fell, providing additional support for equity valuations. The dollar weakened and gold surged 3.3% to $4,710, as safe-haven flows and risk appetite coexisted in a market pricing both de-escalation optimism and lingering inflation uncertainty. Defense Secretary Hegseth noted Iran launched fewer projectiles in the past 24 hours than at any point during the conflict, reinforcing the narrative that the operational tempo is declining.

Consumer confidence edged higher to 91.8 in March, topping the consensus estimate of 87.5 and offering a modest positive amid the prevailing pessimism. The present situation index improved 4.6 points to 123.3. However, the expectations index slipped to 70.9—remaining below the 80 threshold that has historically signaled recession. JOLTS data showed job openings and hirings both declining in February, consistent with a cooling but not collapsing labor market.

Q1 officially closes with the S&P 500 down approximately 7% year-to-date—the weakest first quarter since 2022. The Nasdaq remains in correction territory. Today’s rally does not erase the structural damage of five consecutive losing weeks, but it does suggest that the selling pressure that dominated March has a ceiling when even modest de-escalation signals emerge.

The S&P 500 at 6,529 remains well above the –20% drawdown threshold that would trigger the first buy tranche. The portfolio holds its positioning. Today’s rally confirms the thesis: oversold conditions resolve sharply when the catalyst arrives. The discipline is being in position before it does.
