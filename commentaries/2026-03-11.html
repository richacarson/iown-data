The Session

February CPI landed right on expectations — and the market shrugged. Headline inflation came in at +0.3% MoM and 2.4% YoY, matching consensus. Core CPI rose 0.2% MoM and 2.5% YoY. Rent posted its smallest monthly increase (+0.1%) since January 2021. On its face, this was a constructive print — steady disinflation before the war disrupted everything. But the market faded after the open, with the S&P 500 slipping 0.10% to 6,774.76 and the Dow falling 0.61%.

Traders looked through February’s data and focused on what’s coming. This CPI predates the Iran oil shock — gasoline prices are already up 19% since Feb. 23. Analysts at Carson Group noted that March CPI will tell the real story. Several economists projected headline inflation could reach 3.5% by year-end if oil stays elevated. The Fed meets March 18 and is almost certain to hold. Rate-cut expectations have been pushed to September at the earliest.

The energy trade reasserted itself. Oil ticked higher intraday despite the broader retreat from $119 — Brent near $88. VLO surged 5.73% as refining margins widened further. Gold pulled back 1.05% to $5,187. Bitcoin edged up 0.72%. The VIX dipped 2.45% to 24.32 — the lowest since March 3.

IOWN Tactical: S&P at ~6,775. Still well above our –20% buy trigger (~5,800 from ATH). The February CPI confirms inflation was on track before the war. The question now is how persistent the energy shock will be. PPI tomorrow, FOMC next Wednesday.
