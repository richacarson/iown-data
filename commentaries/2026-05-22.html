The Session

The Dow closed at a fresh record above 50,500 on Friday as a powerful rally across the semiconductor complex carried the IOWN sleeves to a strong finish into the long weekend. The S&P 500 ETF closed at $745.70 (↑0.40%), the Nasdaq proxy at $562.97 (↑0.19%), and the Russell 2000 at $282.49 (↑0.91%), while the Dow proxy added another leg to push to a new all-time high. Volatility eased modestly with the VIX at 16.77, and Treasury yields drifted lower on a defensive backdrop.

The defining feature of the tape was the chip-led narrowing that the morning brief flagged playing out almost exactly as framed. Connectivity, foundry-adjacent, and CPU names ran while Nvidia continued its post-print drift — CRDO ↑12.94% to $218.41, QCOM ↑11.60% to $238.16 in the Dividend sleeve, NXPI ↑5.71% to $316.47, AMD ↑3.99%, MRVL ↑2.96%, ADI ↑3.35% reversing yesterday’s post-print decline, and KEYS ↑2.67%, against NVDA ↓1.90% to $215.33 for its second consecutive regular-session decline after Wednesday’s after-the-close print. Per coverage, the Qualcomm move tied to its strategic push back into the data-center CPU market and a disclosed collaboration with Nvidia on AI cluster solutions — a vote on the AI infrastructure spend cycle, not against it.

The University of Michigan’s final May sentiment reading landed this morning at 44.8, revised down sharply from the 48.2 preliminary and the third straight monthly decline. Per the survey’s director, that mark is the lowest in the index’s history dating to 1952. One-year inflation expectations firmed to 4.8% from the 4.5% preliminary, and five-year expectations rose to 3.9% from 3.4%. Bitcoin gave back ground at $75,807 (↓2.28%), and the IOWN digital-asset sleeve traded with it — IBIT ↓2.36% and ETHA ↓3.59%. Separately, President Trump swore Kevin Warsh in as the new Federal Reserve Chair at the White House this morning, the first such ceremony there since 1987.

A bifurcated chip tape with Nvidia drifting while the layers it anchors hit fresh highs is the clearest read yet that the AI build-out is broader than the headline name. Owning across the stack is what turns that bifurcation into an alpha day rather than a tracking-error problem.
