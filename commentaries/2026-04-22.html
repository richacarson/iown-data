The Session

The risk bid returned with conviction. The S&P 500 climbed 1.05% to a record close at 7,137.90, the Nasdaq added 1.64% to a fresh all-time intraday high, and the Dow tacked on 0.69% to 49,490. Two prior sessions of defensive-led selling were erased in a single day, and the rotation that drove it was the inverse of Tuesday’s tape: AI infrastructure, semiconductors, and crypto led; defense and bond proxies lagged.

Three catalysts converged. President Trump extended the Iran ceasefire indefinitely overnight, removing the binary risk that had weighed on positioning. Pre-market earnings from Boeing and GE Vernova exceeded expectations, with GE Vernova’s data-center power-equipment commentary feeding directly into the AI capex narrative. And on the semiconductor side, AMD surged 6.67%, TSM rose 5.26%, and LRCX gained 2.78% into its post-close print, reaffirming the AI memory-capex thesis the morning brief had flagged as the day’s key read.

Underneath the index strength, dispersion did the work. The Russell 2000 added only 0.74%, the VIX fell 2.97% to 18.92, and Brent crude pushed back above $100 after Iran reportedly seized two container ships in the Strait of Hormuz — the geopolitical risk premium did not fully exit, it simply repriced. Crypto absorbed the cleanest expression of the risk-on shift: Bitcoin rallied 3.85% to $78,540, IBIT gained 5.27%, and the miners ran with it.

Wednesday’s tape was a textbook example of what happens when a defensive-led selloff finds no follow-through — the bid returns to growth and risk assets faster than to the rotations that drove the decline. With the S&P at record levels, both TRIM and BUY lanes are quiet. The work of ownership in this regime is patience and selection, not action.

Holdings News

TEL Q2 print was a beat-and-raise the tape rejected. TE Connectivity reported adjusted EPS of $2.73 on sales of $4.74 billion, both above the prior guide. Record orders of $5.3 billion grew 25% year-over-year with double-digit growth in both Industrial and Transportation segments. Adjusted operating margins expanded 130 basis points to 22%. Q3 guidance for $5 billion in sales (up 10%) and $2.83 EPS (up 17%) cleared consensus. The 9.10% drawdown reflects valuation reset against high expectations rather than an operational concern — the print itself confirms the AI-connectivity and data-center thesis the position was built around.
