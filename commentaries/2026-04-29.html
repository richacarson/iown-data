The Session

The headline tape said almost nothing today; the print tape said almost everything. The S&P 500 finished within a hair of unchanged at 7,137.56 (–0.02%), the Nasdaq Composite added a fractional 0.04% to 24,673.90, and the Dow Industrials slipped 0.57% to 48,861.80. The Russell 2000 lost 0.60%. VIX climbed to 18.36 (+2.97%). Beneath that surface composure, the day was decisively about earnings dispersion, and IOWN’s sleeves landed on the right side of it.

The Federal Reserve held the funds rate at 3.50%–3.75%, the third consecutive hold, in what is very likely Jerome Powell’s final policy decision as chair. Four dissents — the highest since 1992 — split between one dovish vote for a quarter-point cut and three votes to remove the easing bias from the statement. Powell confirmed he intends to remain on the Board of Governors after his chairmanship expires May 15. Kevin Warsh advanced from the Senate Banking Committee on a 13–11 party-line vote this morning. The market reaction was muted: front-end yields ticked up, the long end held, and equities barely moved on the headline.

Where the action lived was in the IOWN reporting calendar. General Dynamics delivered a clean beat-and-raise, ADP raised guidance and authorized a $6 billion buyback, NXP Semiconductors posted what is on track to be its best single session since its 2010 IPO, and Valero added 4.59% on pre-earnings positioning into tomorrow’s print. The Dividend Strategy closed +0.17%, beating DVY (–0.23%) and roughly flat SPY by a full point on the day. The Growth Portfolio added 0.29%, ahead of IUSG (–0.36%) on NXPI’s gap and a recovering AI-infrastructure complex.

Energy held its bid: WTI continues to trade near $100, the Iran-Hormuz standoff remains unresolved, and refiners and integrateds saw fresh interest. Crypto-adjacent names took the day’s losses — HOOD extended its post-earnings decline another 13.24%, and COIN sold off 6.37% in sympathy with Bitcoin slipping to $75,559. The Mag7 reporting block tonight (MSFT, GOOGL, META, AMZN) and QCOM’s after-close print remain the day’s unfinished business.

A flat index print is not a quiet day — it is a day where dispersion does the work. Both sleeves outperformed because owned businesses delivered owned-business results.
