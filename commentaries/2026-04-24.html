The Session

Intel’s after-close print did exactly what the morning brief flagged it would do. Thursday’s blowout from the chipmaker — EPS of $0.29 versus a penny consensus, Q2 revenue guided to a $14.3 billion midpoint against $13.0 billion Street — detonated the entire semiconductor complex on the open and never let go. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index crossed 10,000 for the first time. AMD ripped 13.91% to $347.81 on a D.A. Davidson upgrade to Buy with a $375 target, the analyst writing that CPUs are “reinserting” themselves as foundational to the AI buildout as agentic workloads broaden compute demand beyond GPUs. QCOM, the dividend sleeve’s most-pressured chip name coming into the session, rallied 11.12% to $148.85 — a clean rebuttal to the Barclays Underweight thesis. TSM added 5.17%, NVDA gained 4.32%, CRDO climbed 5.12%, and LRCX advanced 3.57%.

The other thread the brief framed — the weekend Iran escalation window — began closing rather than opening. Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi confirmed travel to Islamabad to meet Pakistani mediators on a second round of US negotiations, and the energy-premium tape that defined Thursday partially unwound. WTI pulled back below $95 even as Brent held above $105. Inside IOWN, the energy sleeve gave back: CVX eased 1.27%, VLO held its bid at +0.86% on continued refining-margin strength. The defense sleeve traded weak in sympathy with the talks momentum — LMT extended Thursday’s miss damage at –3.08% to $513.45, and GD declined 1.73% to $313.21. The dispersion that defined Thursday inverted: defensive sectors that led yesterday’s tape lagged today, and the AI-capex names that absorbed selling reclaimed leadership.

The S&P closed at a record 7,164.29. The Nasdaq printed a fresh all-time high of 24,836.60. The Dow finished modestly red at –0.16% on the defensive rotation reversal. VIX fell 3.42% to 18.65. Michigan Sentiment’s one-year inflation expectation came in at 4.7 versus 4.8 consensus — a narrow miss to the cool side that softened the fear of cost-push transmission to household psychology, and a constructive setup heading into next week’s April 28–29 FOMC meeting. The week ends with the S&P up roughly 0.6% and the Nasdaq up 1.5%, both indices having fully recovered the Iran-war drawdown and now extended past prior highs.

A tape where chip names lead records back while energy and defense unwind tells the committee where capital is being repositioned. The AI-capex thread is broadening, not narrowing.
