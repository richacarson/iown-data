The Session

Tuesday’s defensive rotation reversed cleanly. The April Producer Price Index landed materially above consensus — headline PPI rose 1.4% month-over-month and 6.0% year-over-year per coverage from 24/7 Wall St. and TheStreet, the steepest wholesale print since 2022 and well above the 0.5% MoM consensus. Core PPI rose 1.0% MoM versus 0.4% expected. The tape absorbed the print and turned higher: the S&P 500 closed at 7,444.88 (+0.59%) and the Nasdaq at 26,402.34 (+1.20%), with the Dow flat at −0.14%. Treasury yields ground higher into 2026 highs — the 10-year near 4.47% per Edward Jones’ midday note and the long end above 5% per TheStreet — but equities priced the inflation reset as background to the day’s organizing event.

That event was Jensen Huang’s last-minute addition to Trump’s Beijing delegation. Per CNBC and Yahoo Finance reporting, the NVIDIA CEO flew to Anchorage to board Air Force One on the refueling stop after President Trump personally called following coverage of Huang’s initial absence from the list. He joins Tesla’s Musk, Apple’s Cook, Boeing’s Ortberg, and roughly fifteen other executives ahead of the May 14–15 summit. The tape read the inclusion as a credible test of whether the H200 export-license logjam can be relieved bilaterally; NVDA closed at $225.83 (+2.29%) and the connectivity layer extended into the session — MRVL at $177.95 (+8.18%) on top of yesterday’s contribution and FTNT at $117.69 (+3.35%) on the NVIDIA-Fortinet-Red Hat secure enterprise AI partnership announcement.

The defensive cohort that led Tuesday gave back. TEL at $207.66 (−2.84%) and SSNC at $64.77 (−2.91%) absorbed the rotation, ADP at $208.63 (−2.42%) softened on no specific catalyst, and the rate-sensitive utilities held flat to lower (NEE +0.27%, ATO −1.09%, EIX −0.91%). The dividend sleeve closed essentially unchanged at −0.05% — a result that beat DVY’s −0.41% on the day even as it trailed SPY’s +0.56%, the precise signature of the strategy in a session where mega-cap tech is the engine.

The IOWN framework was built for a no-cut, energy-impulse regime. The dividend sleeve maintains its YTD lead over both benchmarks (12.54% vs DVY 7.72% / SPY 8.86%) and the growth portfolio remains within 39 basis points of IUSG without owning the mega-cap concentration. Think Like an Owner.
