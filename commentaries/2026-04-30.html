The Session

The catalyst stack the morning brief teed up resolved squarely higher. Four hyperscaler prints, an FOMC hold, the first post-Iran-war Q1 GDP advance, and the Fed’s preferred inflation gauge all cleared inside 24 hours, and the S&P 500 rose 1.08% to 7,213.32, touching a fresh intraday high before settling. The Russell 2000 added 2.21%, the Dow climbed 1.62%, and the Nasdaq Composite finished up 0.95%. Volatility collapsed in tandem — the VIX printed 16.93, down 9.99% on the session as the catalyst-anxiety bid compressed.

The composition of the rally was the story. Alphabet’s blowout (Google Cloud +63% to $20.03B, total revenue $109.9B, EPS $5.11 against $2.62 consensus) and Caterpillar’s Q1 print drove the tape; Meta and Microsoft weighed on the Nasdaq as their raised AI capex guides triggered a multi-day rerating of free-cash-flow assumptions. The Magnificent Seven split in two: the cloud platforms with contracted backlog (GOOGL, AMZN) were rewarded, the platforms increasing capex without commensurate near-term monetization (META –10%, MSFT –5%) were not. Apple reports tonight as the last print of the cycle.

Q1 GDP came in at 2.0% annualized, below the 2.2% consensus but a sharp acceleration from Q4’s 0.5%, with investment, government spending, and exports the principal contributors. Core PCE for March printed 3.2% year-over-year, in line with expectations and accelerating from 3.0%, with the headline PCE jumping to 3.5% on the energy pass-through. Initial jobless claims fell to 189,000 for the week ended April 25 — the lowest weekly print since 1969. The labor market remains the tightest datapoint in the macro dashboard.

Brent touched $126.41 intraday, the highest since March 2022, before retreating roughly 3.5% as profit-taking and a sharp dollar weakening (yen +3% on intervention warnings) capped the move. WTI gave back 2.21% to $104.52. Both benchmarks remain on track for a fourth consecutive monthly gain, but the inability to hold the four-year high is the first tactical signal that the energy bid has stretched.

A session in which the Dividend Strategy beat both DVY and SPY by meaningful margins, while the Growth Portfolio captured the day’s rotation cleanly, is exactly the dispersion that concentrated, fundamentally-anchored portfolios are built to harvest. Compounders win days like this.

Holdings News

QCOM (Q2 fiscal earnings). Cristiano Amon confirmed on the call that initial data-center custom-silicon shipments to a leading hyperscaler are on track for later this calendar year, with a multi-generation engagement framework. The board authorized a fresh $20B buyback on top of the existing program, with $5.4B already completed in the first half of fiscal 2026. Q2 returned $3.7B to shareholders ($945M dividends, $2.8B buybacks). Investor day scheduled for June 24 will provide more detail on the data-center and physical-AI growth initiatives.

CAT (Q1 earnings). Adjusted operating margin expanded 30 bps to 18.0% on $17.4B sales. Construction Industries +38%, Resource Industries +4%, Power & Energy +22%. Power-generation revenue specifically rose 41% to $2.82B with most of that growth tied directly to data-center demand. Backlog of $63B (+79% YoY) provides multi-year visibility. Management is expanding large-reciprocating-engine capacity to nearly 3x the 2024 level, with capital deployment concentrated in 2027–2029. Long-term sales CAGR through 2030 raised to 6–9% from 5–7%; 2030 power-generation sales target raised to more than 3x the 2024 baseline.

MARA (M&A). Announced a $1.5B agreement to acquire Long Ridge Energy from FTAI Infrastructure — a 505 MW combined-cycle gas turbine facility on the Ohio River in the PJM Interconnection territory. MARA already operates a 200 MW co-located data center on the Long Ridge site. The deal lifts total owned-and-operated power capacity 65% to approximately 2.2 GW across PJM, ERCOT, SPP, and international markets, and adds about $144M in annualized adjusted EBITDA. Closing expected in the second half of 2026 pending regulatory approvals; AI data-center service targeted for mid-2028. CEO Fred Thiel: “Power is the scarce input in AI.”

GD (guidance raise). General Dynamics raised 2026 EPS guidance on Marine Systems strength, with the submarine backlog continuing to deliver above prior expectations. The defense-budget framework reported earlier this week was characterized as supportive of the multi-year program ramp, particularly Columbia-class.
