The Session

Equities edged higher Tuesday as buyers emerged after last week’s selling, though the session lacked real conviction. The S&P 500 added 0.27% to 6,717, the Dow rose 0.10%, and the Nasdaq gained 0.47%. Small-caps outpaced with the Russell 2000 up 0.67%. The VIX dipped 4.8% to 22.38—still elevated, but the first meaningful downtick in over a week.

The macro backdrop remains dominated by the Middle East conflict. Attacks on the UAE’s Fujairah port and Shah gas field added to energy supply concerns, and Gulf states are pressing Washington to neutralize Iran’s military capabilities as the Strait of Hormuz crisis deepens. US average diesel crossed $5 per gallon for the first time in this cycle, a real headwind for transportation and manufacturing margins. Brent crude held above $100. The number of US troops wounded in the conflict has risen to approximately 200.

Despite those headwinds, today’s bounce felt more like short-covering and positioning than a fundamental shift. Volume was unremarkable. Semiconductors showed relative strength—Lam Research up 3.2%, TSM up 1.7%—ahead of Nvidia’s GTC conference. Energy names benefited from the sustained crude bid. Defensives were mixed, with Lockheed Martin off 1.4% even as geopolitical tensions escalate.

Gold added 0.16% and continues to trade around $5,010. Bitcoin slipped 0.5% to $74,520, largely range-bound as digital assets consolidate near recent levels.

The S&P remains well within its normal range relative to the 200-day moving average. No tactical signals triggered. We continue to own quality and collect dividends through the volatility.
