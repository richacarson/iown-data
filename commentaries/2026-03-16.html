The Session

Wall Street snapped a three-week losing streak on Monday as easing Strait of Hormuz concerns and Nvidia’s GTC conference lifted risk appetite across the board. The S&P 500 rose 1.21% to 6,712.54, reclaiming ground after Friday’s close marked the index’s lowest level of 2026. The Dow added 1.25% to 47,139, while the Nasdaq gained 1.23% to 22,374. The Russell 2000 advanced 0.94% to 2,503.

The catalyst was weekend news that select tankers carrying liquefied petroleum gas successfully transited the Strait of Hormuz, suggesting a degree of Iranian leniency toward allied energy shipments. Brent crude pulled back roughly 2.4% to around $102 per barrel after touching $105 earlier in the session. WTI fell 4.1% to $94.62. Treasury Secretary Bessent reportedly indicated the U.S. is facilitating a coalition to escort commercial vessels through the waterway, which further eased the energy supply premium that had been building for weeks.

Semiconductors led the session with the VanEck Semiconductor ETF rising 1.7%. Nvidia gained over 2% ahead of CEO Jensen Huang’s GTC keynote, where he projected $1 trillion in purchase orders for the Blackwell and Vera Rubin chip platforms through 2027—double the company’s prior estimate. Meta rose nearly 3% after reports that the company plans to lay off up to 20% of its workforce in an efficiency push tied to AI adoption. Credit-sensitive sectors benefited as yields retreated across the curve.

The S&P 500 remains roughly 4% below its recent high. No tactical triggers are in play. Today’s bounce was constructive, but the index would need sustained follow-through above 6,750 to suggest the correction has fully stabilized. Ownership means accepting that relief rallies and renewed pullbacks are both part of the process.
