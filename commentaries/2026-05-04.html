The Session

The pre-market tape did not survive the open. Friday closed at fresh all-time highs near 7,187 on the S&P 500, and the overnight read pointed to a tech-led continuation: XLK indicated +1.49%, CRDO bid +5.96%, AMD +1.71% into Tuesday’s print. By the close, that sequencing had inverted. The S&P finished –0.40% at 7,201.32, the Dow shed –1.13% as cyclicals and industrials led lower, the Nasdaq cushioned at –0.19%, and the VIX advanced +7% to 18.18. Project Freedom commenced as scheduled, but Brent absorbed the convoy news without a meaningful reaction in either direction.

The day’s defining move came inside the AI semiconductor complex. AMD closed –5.39% at $341.11 ahead of its Tuesday-after-close print, with HSBC issuing a downgrade Monday morning and the broader narrative shifting toward what 24/7 Wall St. termed a fracturing of the unified AI chip trade. NVDA finished essentially unchanged at +0.05%, INTC was modestly lower, and AMD bore the entire selling pressure on its own. The setup matters: AMD enters the print with consensus near $1.28 EPS on $9.88B revenue, against company guidance of approximately $9.8B and 55% non-GAAP gross margins. The question the print answers is not whether AMD beats — it is whether the sequential decline gets framed as seasonality or softness.

Underneath, the divergence ran the other direction in crypto. Bitcoin reclaimed $80,000 on a confluence of Senate stablecoin compromise news and renewed CLARITY Act momentum, with Senators Tillis and Alsobrooks reaching bipartisan agreement on stablecoin yield provisions. IBIT advanced +2.09%, ETHA +2.01%, COIN +6.14% into its Wednesday print, HOOD +3.92%, MARA +3.23%, HUT +1.43%. The same session that punished AI hardware rewarded the digital-asset complex — the cleanest read on rotation rather than risk-off.

New York Fed President Williams delivered a Star-Wars-themed address (“There Is No Try”) at the Council on Foreign Relations, closing with “May the 4th be with you.” The substance: monetary policy remains well positioned, the FOMC’s April 29 hold at 3.50–3.75% reflects appropriate calibration, and the Middle East conflict represents the principal substantial risk in the outlook. Factory Orders for March printed +1.5% MoM versus +0.5% consensus — a meaningful upside surprise that did nothing to lift the tape. Total Vehicle Sales came in at 15.9M against 16.0M consensus.

The session resolved the morning’s open question by going the other way. Pre-market AI strength gave way to AI dispersion; convoy day produced no oil reaction; and crypto carried the digital-asset sleeve while the equity complex sorted itself out by name.
