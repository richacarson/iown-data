The Session

The index ended Monday roughly where it began; the semiconductor sleeve did not. The S&P 500 closed at 7,412.95 (+0.19%), the Dow at 49,704.48 (+0.19%), and the Nasdaq at 26,267.79 (+0.08%) — all marginally green, all near record territory. The Russell 2000 led the major averages at +0.33%. Beneath that placid surface, breadth was narrower than the tape suggested, with more than half of NYSE issues declining. The VIX rose 6.81% to 18.36, a quiet warning that the calm at the index level is being purchased with rising hedging demand.

The day belonged to one stock. Qualcomm gained 8.42% to $237.53 after CEO Cristiano Amon disclosed that the company’s data center processors are on track to ship to a major hyperscaler before the end of calendar 2026. That single line reframed Qualcomm from a handset and licensing business into a credible participant in AI infrastructure. QCOM was the single best performer in both the S&P 500 and the Nasdaq 100. CRDO compounded the move in the same direction, gaining 11.52% to $210.22 as the optical connectivity layer of the same build-out continued to rerate.

The Morning Brief opened the week with AMD’s Q1 print as proof that AI infrastructure demand persists. Today extended the answer: the demand is not concentrated in one name, and the connectivity, networking, and edge-compute layers around the GPUs are now drawing their own bid. The headline indices barely moved because the gains were narrow. The portfolio moved considerably more because the names that ran are exactly the ones it owns.

Concentration in the AI infrastructure complex is the intentional design of the portfolio, not an accident of the rally. Days like today are what that design is for.

Holdings News

QCOM — Hyperscaler Data Center Deal Confirmed. CEO Cristiano Amon disclosed that Qualcomm’s custom data center processors will ship to a major hyperscaler customer before the end of calendar 2026. The company also raised $20 billion in new share repurchase authorization on top of $2.1 billion still unused from prior plans, and raised the quarterly dividend. The next major catalyst is the June 24 Investor Day, where management is expected to outline the scope of the data center opportunity in detail.

CTRA — Delisted Following Devon Merger Close. Coterra Energy completed its all-stock merger with Devon Energy on May 7 and has been delisted from the NYSE. The combined entity carries an estimated $58 billion enterprise value with projected $1 billion in annual synergies and a 33% dividend increase. The position will reflect the new Devon Energy shares going forward.
