The Session

The second quarter closed where it spent its final stretch — with semiconductors in front. The S&P 500 added 0.75% to 7,496.31 and the Nasdaq rose 1.48% to 26,203.18, while the Dow gained 0.26% to 52,319.20, holding above the 52,000 mark first reached Monday on Alphabet’s debut in the index. Per Reuters and Yahoo Finance, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq posted their strongest quarter since 2020. The VIX eased 6.74% to 16.46.

The chip cohort did the heavy lifting. Within our sleeves, Credo closed up 10.69%, AMD added 7.68%, and Marvell rose 7.25%, with Lam Research up 5.46% and Taiwan Semiconductor up 4.94%. The move extended the single-session repricing that began Monday rather than fading into quarter-end profit-taking — the open question coming into the day, now answered. Reported large-scale South Korean capacity commitments from Samsung and SK Hynix supported the equipment names, and BlackRock’s note on a broadening AI trade beyond the largest chipmakers added breadth to the bid.

The macro backdrop was constructive. A May JOLTS reading on job openings came in better than expected per Yahoo Finance, setting up Thursday’s pulled-forward June payrolls report ahead of the July 3 market closure. A Supreme Court ruling left Federal Reserve independence intact for now, and U.S. envoy Steve Witkoff arrived in Doha for Tuesday talks with Iranian counterparts. Crude finished the quarter with its steepest quarterly decline since 2020 per Reuters, a tailwind for consumers even as it pressured the energy names.

With the S&P 500 well above Carson’s SPY support at $645.80 (set April 13), no TRIM or BUY trigger is in play. Quarter-end mark-ups and index reconstitution are calendar mechanics, not signals to adjust ownership. The physical world matters more than the print.

Holdings News

Stryker — index reconstitution. Stryker was removed from the Russell Growth Index in the quarter-end reconstitution, prompting forced selling from index-tracking funds, with the stock also trading ex-dividend Tuesday. The move is mechanical rather than fundamental; sell-side coverage remains constructive, with a Buy consensus and recent targets in the high $300s.

Credo — analyst momentum. Credo drew a fresh wave of bullish revisions, including BofA lifting its target to $340 and Evercore ISI initiating at Outperform with a $325 target, citing accelerating demand for high-speed AI data-center connectivity.

Taiwan Semiconductor — target raised. Per Fintel, TSM’s price target was increased roughly 14% to near $497, reflecting continued conviction in foundry demand across the AI build-out.
