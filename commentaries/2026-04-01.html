The Session

Equities extended their relief rally into a second session on Wednesday, opening Q2 with broad gains as ceasefire rhetoric intensified. The S&P 500 added 0.71% to 6,575, the Nasdaq gained 1.16%, and the Dow rose 0.48%. The catalyst was familiar: President Trump posted on Truth Social that Iran’s president had asked for a ceasefire, adding that the US would only consider it when the Strait of Hormuz is “open, free, and clear.” Tehran’s foreign ministry called the claim “false and baseless.”

The pattern from Tuesday repeated—markets rallied on the aspiration of de-escalation while the diplomatic substance remained unresolved. Brent crude fell sharply, briefly touching below $100 per barrel for the first time since early March before settling near $102. The energy sector lost more than 4% on the session, reversing weeks of war-premium gains. Growth and technology led, with semiconductors, gold miners, and industrials at the front of the rotation.

Economic data provided a constructive backdrop. ISM Manufacturing registered 52.7 in March, beating the 52.3 consensus and marking a third consecutive month of expansion. Production rose to 55.1 and new orders held at 53.5. The caution flag was the prices-paid subindex, which surged 7.8 points to 78.3—the highest reading since June 2022—reflecting energy-driven input cost pressure across the industrial base. ADP reported 62,000 private-sector jobs added in March, above the 40,000 consensus, though growth remained narrowly concentrated in healthcare and construction. Gold surged 2.47% to $4,794, extending its rally as both safe-haven demand and ceasefire optimism supported the metal simultaneously.

The S&P at 6,575 remains well within the ownership zone. Two consecutive sessions of gains on exit rhetoric are encouraging, but Iran’s denial of the ceasefire request repeats the pattern—rallying on what Trump says, not what Iran agrees to. Tonight’s 9 PM presidential address is the week’s binary event. The ISM prices-paid print at 78.3 is the data point that persists regardless of the geopolitical outcome.
