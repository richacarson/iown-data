The Session

The de-rating did not extend. Tuesday closed with the chip complex caught in a two-front reprice — a Samsung earnings miss against a bar the buy-side had set too high, and a report that China’s DeepSeek is developing its own inference chip. The open question into Wednesday was whether that was one bad session or the front end of something wider. The market answered it directly: the sleeve snapped back rather than following through. Nvidia led it, closing up 3.65% at $204.12 as one of the few names the tape wanted to own, and the rest of the complex came with it — Credo +4.99%, NXP +3.93%, Keysight +2.63%, Lam Research +2.15%, Qualcomm +1.96%, Taiwan Semiconductor +1.02%. A one-day de-rating stayed a one-day de-rating.

What the chips could not do was carry the index. The S&P 500 closed –0.29% at 7,481.75 and the Dow gave back 1.09% to 52,348, with the Russell 2000 off 0.88%; only the Nasdaq finished green, and only just, up 0.19% on Nvidia’s bid. Breadth, in other words, was heavy beneath a recovering headline. The overhang was geopolitical. On the record from the Ankara NATO summit, the President declared the late-June Iran framework ‘over,’ and the S&P dipped on the headline. Crude extended its Strait-of-Hormuz premium into the statement, and the energy names carried the day’s clearest offset — Valero +6.26%, Devon +2.12%, Chevron +1.10%, ONEOK +0.54%.

The second current ran through rates. Into the first Warsh-chaired FOMC minutes — the committee’s only on-record statement on a possible September hike after the Chair sat out the June dot plot — the rate-sensitive corners bore the weight. Synchrony fell 9.61% and Toll Brothers 4.02%, consumer-credit and housing proxies both, while the utilities softened. The VIX firmed 2.85% to 16.59: a lean, not a spasm. Gold eased 1.60% and Bitcoin slipped to $62,177, dragging the digital-asset ETFs lower with it (IBIT –2.54%, ETHA –2.96%).

A session like this is where a diversified book earns its keep. The leadership that broke Tuesday healed Wednesday, and the ballast — energy, healthcare, quality dividends — held the line while the rate-sensitive names took the blow. We own both sleeves so the portfolio, not the day’s loudest headline, sets the pace. With the S&P still within a percent of Monday’s record and far above the spring lows, the framework keeps us trimming and building cash, not buying — this is not the –20% first tranche.
