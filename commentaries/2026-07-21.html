The Session

The tentative chip-cohort bounce the morning could only sketch resolved, by the close, into a broad advance — and the portfolio’s growth sleeve captured it in full. After last week carried the semiconductor complex into a bear market on doubts about the durability of AI spending, Monday steadied the group and Tuesday confirmed it: a Morgan Stanley call that data-center demand will keep memory pricing firm sent memory names higher, and the broader chip complex carried the tape from there. The S&P 500 added 0.86% to 7,507, the Nasdaq 1.26%, and the Russell 2000 1.53% — leadership from the highest-beta corner of the market rather than its defensive core. The VIX fell better than nine percent to 16.91, the clearest sign that last week’s growth scare has, for now, drained away.

For our books, the session was a study in dispersion. The Growth Portfolio gained 2.87%, better than doubling IUSG’s 1.28%, as the merchant-silicon and AI-infrastructure names we own did the heavy lifting — AMD (+8.11%), Marvell (+6.68%), Taiwan Semiconductor (+5.55%), Credo (+5.56%) and, on the value side, Lam Research (+4.97%). The Dividend Strategy, by contrast, slipped 0.17% as the staples and quality-compounder names that anchor it — S&P Global (–3.81%), ADP (–3.53%), Church & Dwight and Colgate — funded the rotation into risk. It was the mirror image of a defensive day, and a reminder that a portfolio built to own both halves of the market rarely has both working at once.

Beneath the equity bid, the geopolitical file moved the other way. The morning’s hope for a ten-day Hormuz ceasefire faded as Iran declared the framework collapsed, U.S. forces completed a tenth consecutive night of strikes, and Yemen’s Houthis declared a blockade on Saudi-linked shipping — sending at least two Saudi crude tankers into U-turns in the Red Sea. Brent reclaimed $90.90, up roughly 1.9% and its highest in five weeks. Our upstream names took it in stride rather than in celebration: Chevron added 0.72% and CNX 3.00%, while ONEOK eased 1.85% — energy was a passenger today, not the driver. The Fed’s pre-meeting blackout continues into the July 28–29 decision, leaving the earnings gauntlet and the Iran cadence to set the tone.

A single risk-on session is the market testing a thesis, not settling it — and the real read still runs through this week’s megacap prints. The tactical framework calls for no action here: the tranche-buying levels sit far below current prices, and the day’s rotation is noise around businesses that keep compounding. We stay owners of the whole portfolio.

Holdings News

Genuine Parts (GPC) — Q2 beat, guidance reaffirmed. Second-quarter revenue rose 6% year-over-year to $6.54 billion, above the $6.43 billion consensus, and adjusted EPS of $2.15 topped the $2.08 estimate. Management reaffirmed full-year adjusted-EPS guidance of $7.50 to $8.00 and reiterated that the planned separation into two public companies remains on track for the first quarter of 2027; same-store sales rose 3.4%. The shares eased on the day as risk appetite favored cyclicals and semiconductors over the defensive book.

NVIDIA (NVDA) — stake in Nebius. NVIDIA rose nearly 2% after disclosing an investment in neocloud provider Nebius, extending its practice of seeding the AI-infrastructure ecosystem it supplies. The move reinforced the capital-recycling dynamic that has kept the merchant-silicon complex central to the market’s leadership heading into this week’s megacap prints.
