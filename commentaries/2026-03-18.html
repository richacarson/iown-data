The Session

Stocks sold off sharply Wednesday after the Federal Reserve held rates steady and Chair Jerome Powell underscored that higher energy prices will push inflation above the committee’s target for longer than expected. The S&P 500 fell 1.37% to 5,624.38, the Dow dropped 1.63%, the Nasdaq declined 1.46%, and the Russell 2000 lost 1.64%. Markets had been under pressure all morning after the February Producer Price Index came in at +0.7% month-over-month, more than double the 0.3% consensus.

The FOMC voted 10–2 to keep the federal funds rate at 3.50–3.75%. Governors Miran and Waller dissented in favor of a quarter-point cut. The updated dot plot still projects one reduction this year and another in 2027, but seven of nineteen participants now see no cuts at all in 2026—one more than December. Core PCE forecasts were revised up to 2.7% from 2.5%. Powell acknowledged the Fed has not made as much progress on inflation as hoped and said the central bank needs to see goods inflation, boosted by tariffs and now energy costs, ease before resuming cuts.

Brent crude settled above $104, extending its war-driven rally as Iran warned Gulf energy sites to evacuate after its own gas facilities were struck. The 10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.26%. Gold fell 3.6% to $4,826 as a stronger dollar and margin liquidation overwhelmed safe-haven demand. Bitcoin dropped 4.0% to $70,956. The VIX climbed 12% to 25.09.

The S&P 500 remains well above our first deployment threshold at –20% from the all-time high. With inflation reaccelerating and the Fed signaling patience, the current posture is to hold positions and let compounding do its work. Days like today are the cost of admission for long-term ownership.
