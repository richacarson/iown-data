The Session

Both books finished the day in the green; the cap-weighted indices did not. That single line is the story of June 22. The Dividend Strategy added 0.07% and the Growth Portfolio gained 0.98% on a session where the S&P 500 slipped 0.33% and the Nasdaq fell 1.29%. The drag was concentrated and identifiable: Alphabet shed roughly 5% and the rest of the AI hyperscaler cohort gave back ground on renewed questions about the scale of capital spending. Because those names dominate the cap-weighted tape and are largely absent from our two books, the indices and our portfolios pointed in opposite directions.

Underneath the megacap headline, the market was broader and healthier than the index print suggested. The Dow rose 0.29%, the Russell 2000 added 0.83%, and the chip complex that carried Thursday’s pre-holiday recovery simply kept going. Lam Research advanced 5.27%, Analog Devices 2.54%, NXP 3.18% and AMD 2.37%, while Credo led the entire portfolio with an 11.29% move on a wave of analyst price-target increases. Caterpillar tacked on 3.70% to lead the Dow’s industrials. These are the businesses we own — connectivity, semis, machinery — and they did the work today.

The energy and defense sleeves resolved exactly along the position-symmetric lines we framed into the open. With the U.S. and Iran reporting encouraging progress and a 60-day roadmap, crude eased and Washington authorized Iranian oil sales — yet Valero (+3.17%), Devon (+2.24%), ONEOK (+1.48%) and Chevron (+0.67%) held firm on cash-flow durability rather than the spot barrel. The reciprocal trade showed up in defense, where Lockheed gave back 3.40% and General Dynamics 1.90% on the same easing-escalation read. The digital sleeve firmed with Bitcoin near $64.5K: IBIT +2.47%, ETHA +1.40%.

When the index falls on three or four names and the portfolio rises on twenty, that is not luck — it is the structure of owning businesses rather than a concentration. We let the tape do the diverging.
