The Session

Markets snapped back from Monday’s selloff with the chip complex doing the heavy lifting. The S&P 500 added 0.83% to 7,260, the Nasdaq Composite gained 1.03% to a fresh record at 25,326, and the Russell 2000 surged 1.75% to 2,845. Energy unwound yesterday’s spike: Brent fell nearly 4% to $109.87 and WTI lost a similar amount to $102.27 after Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed the Iran ceasefire “certainly holds” and the US escorted two commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz with destroyer cover. The VIX dropped 4.70% to 17.43.

Semiconductors led from open to close. Qualcomm extended last week’s post-earnings rally with a 10.80% advance to $186.56 as reports surfaced of a partnership with OpenAI to develop an AI-native smartphone chip targeting 2028 production. The move builds on the late-April hyperscaler shipping disclosure that drove the stock’s initial gap higher. Lam Research climbed 6.66% to $275.80 — a fresh high — with analyst price targets continuing to ratchet upward following its April beat-and-raise. AMD recovered 4.02% from Monday’s pre-earnings selloff and reports after the close tonight, with consensus near $1.29 EPS on $9.84 billion in revenue and data center expected at $5.56 billion.

Industrials and materials joined the move. Caterpillar added 3.41% to $904.59 as the AI data center power thesis extended, and Steel Dynamics gained 3.32%. The JOLTS report showed March job openings at 6.87 million, modestly above estimates, but the more notable detail was hiring jumping 655,000 to 5.55 million — the highest hires rate since May 2024. The print suggests the low-hire, low-fire equilibrium that has defined this labor market may be shifting. April ISM Services PMI came in at 53.6, marginally below estimates but firmly in expansion territory.

The morning’s open question was whether the index could hold these levels on fundamentals alone now that positioning normalization is largely complete. Today answered affirmatively. The real test arrives in roughly an hour, when AMD prints data center revenue.

Holdings News

COIN announces 14% workforce reduction. Coinbase disclosed plans to cut approximately 700 employees, with a $50–$60 million restructuring charge expected in Q2. CEO Brian Armstrong framed the move as both a response to the crypto market downturn and an acceleration toward AI-native operations: smaller, flatter teams with managers required to function as “player-coaches.” Q1 earnings report follows Thursday after the close.

AMD reports Q1 after the close tonight. Consensus is approximately $1.29 EPS on $9.84 billion in revenue, with data center expected at $5.56 billion (roughly +52% year-over-year). Options markets are pricing an 8% post-earnings move. Gross margin guidance at 55% is the secondary watch point. The result will set the tone for the entire IOWN semiconductor sleeve.
