The Session

Monday’s hope trade met Tuesday’s reality. The S&P 500 fell 0.36% to 6,557, paring a decline that had approached 1% intraday before President Trump told reporters the U.S. and Iran are “in negotiations right now.” The Dow edged down 0.18% to 46,124 and the Nasdaq slid 0.84% to 21,762. The Russell 2000, by contrast, added 0.45%—a small-cap resilience that echoed Monday’s risk-on bid but diverged sharply from the large-cap tech weakness that defined Tuesday’s tape.

The session’s character was set before the open. Iran’s parliament speaker dismissed Trump’s claims of productive talks as an attempt to manipulate oil markets. Brent crude climbed back above $104, erasing much of Monday’s 11% decline and confirming the skepticism our pre-market analysis flagged. An explosion at Valero’s Port Arthur refinery in Texas—still burning Tuesday morning—added upward pressure to gasoline and diesel futures, though VLO itself rose 1.84% as refining margins widened for competitors. The VIX ticked up 3.06% to 26.95.

Beneath the index-level softness, the tape was bifurcated. Semiconductors and industrials extended Monday’s momentum: KEYS +3.33%, ADI +3.09%, TEL +2.78%, CAT +2.13%. Crypto-linked names reversed sharply as proposed stablecoin legislation surfaced and Argus lowered COIN estimates: COIN fell 9.76%, MARA 7.41%, HOOD 4.70%. The digital asset sleeve that led Monday gave back those gains and then some. Gold rebounded 1.23% to $4,461, a notable reversal after its extended losing streak—the kind of session that tends to mark at least a near-term floor.

The S&P 500 remains below its 200-day moving average and roughly 7% off January’s 7,002 peak. The five-day diplomatic window through Saturday offers binary risk: concrete progress extends the relief trade, failure to produce a framework returns us to the war premium. We remain positioned through the range of outcomes.

Holdings News

VLO — Port Arthur Refinery Explosion. An explosion at Valero’s Port Arthur, Texas facility Monday evening was still burning Tuesday morning. The diesel-producing unit is expected to be offline for an extended period, while the gasoline unit may restart within weeks. Wholesale gasoline futures rose roughly 10 cents per gallon and diesel 16 cents on the incident. VLO shares rose 1.84% to $241.75 as the disruption widened crack spreads for the broader refining complex.

COIN — Stablecoin Legislation Threat. Reports surfaced of proposed legislation that could restrict stablecoin operations, a key revenue driver for Coinbase. Stablecoin revenue reached $1.34 billion in 2025. Argus simultaneously reiterated a Hold rating and lowered earnings estimates.

ARM Chip Launch — Read-Through for QCOM. Arm Holdings announced plans to sell its own chips, a move that could make some of its current licensees into competitors. QCOM rose a modest 0.25% as markets assess implications for Qualcomm’s licensing arrangements.
