The Session

The broad tape held green while its leadership quietly changed hands. The S&P 500 closed +0.36% and the Nasdaq +0.60% as mega-cap technology and defensive sectors carried the index, but the day’s real story was a rotation out of semiconductors. Apple, Alphabet, Amazon and Microsoft each advanced roughly 3–4%, absorbing capital that flowed out of the chip complex.

A second soft inflation print did the macro work. June producer prices fell 0.3% against expectations for no change, reinforcing Tuesday’s cooler CPI. New York Fed President John Williams pointed to encouraging progress on inflation, and the VIX eased to 15.69. ASML raised its 2026 revenue guidance for the second time this year, which briefly lifted equipment names before the sector reversed. Reports that SK Hynix is scaling back HBM4 output — tied to softer demand for Nvidia’s next-generation Rubin chips — combined with fresh China-competition concerns to trigger broad profit-taking across chips that had run hundreds of percent this year. Warren Buffett, separately, offered a pointed reminder about speculative excess.

The rotation cut cleanly along our two sleeve lines. The Dividend Strategy — anchored in financials, staples and healthcare, and largely insulated from AI hardware — gained +0.17%, edging DVY (+0.08%) while trailing a broad-tape SPY (+0.38%). The Growth Portfolio’s semiconductor concentration bore the pullback, closing –0.34% against IUSG’s +0.55%. Worth noting: the mega-cap chip anchors were largely spared — NVDA held +0.33% and TSM slipped just –0.22% — while the higher-beta names took the profit-taking.

Concentrated leadership cuts both ways, and today it cut against us. We own businesses, not the tape. A single-session handback in positions up triple digits year-to-date is the market catching its breath — not a thesis breaking.
