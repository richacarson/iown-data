The Session

The de-leveraging that defined Friday reversed cleanly Monday. The S&P 500 closed +0.30% at 7,405.81, the Nasdaq +0.86% to 25,929.40, and the Russell 2000 +0.77%, with the Dow the lone laggard at –0.16%. The VIX fell 13.11% to 18.69, unwinding most of Friday’s volatility spike. The shape of the tape was a near-mirror of the prior session: the leadership that compressed hardest Friday led the recovery, and the defensive names that held the line gave a measured portion back.

The semiconductor complex carried the move. The names that de-rated 7–17% Friday rebounded across the board — AMD +5.14%, MRVL +9.63%, TSM +2.80%, NVDA +1.73%, NXPI +1.75%, with QCOM back to green at +0.85%. CRDO added +7.43%. Crypto recovered in tandem: Bitcoin +1.85% to $63,286, IBIT +5.13%, ETHA +7.16%, and the crypto-adjacent equity sleeve rallied with it — MARA +11.85%, HUT +6.56%, COIN +6.37%, HOOD +3.12%.

The defensive and dividend backbone that anchored Friday’s green tally gave a portion back into the risk-on rotation: CL –2.83%, CHD –1.44%, NEE –2.13%, DGX –1.54%, SYK –1.35%, TEL –3.31%. Energy held firm against the move on the geopolitical backdrop — DVN +1.81%, CVX +1.03%, VLO +1.00%. Gold eased –0.33% to $4,350.70.

On the geopolitical front, Iran and Israel said they had halted strikes on each other for now, per Reuters mid-session — a de-escalation that pulled back some of the weekend’s physical-infrastructure premium, even as the US disabled an Iran-bound tanker in the Gulf of Oman and the EU sanctioned Iranian figures over naval restrictions in the Strait of Hormuz. Traders quoted by CNBC do not expect Hormuz traffic to normalize until year-end.

One session does not reset a thesis any more than the prior one did. Friday’s synchronized compression and Monday’s synchronized rebound both fit a positioning-unwind read rather than a macro break — the committee compounds through Wednesday’s CPI window, not around it. Think Like an Owner.
