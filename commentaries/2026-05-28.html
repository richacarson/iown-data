The Session

The index closed at a new record, and it did so against a tape that gave it every reason not to. The S&P 500 rose 0.57% to 7,563, the Nasdaq added 0.90%, and the Dow held essentially flat. The advance settled the question that had been hanging over the market all week — an index pinned near its highs while sentiment sat in extreme fear — firmly on the side of price.

Two catalysts carried the day. Washington and Tehran agreed to a 60-day memorandum extending the ceasefire, pending final approval, which let the energy and risk complexes exhale. And a blowout outlook from a major enterprise-software name reignited the entire software group, with the sector ETF up nearly 3%. The semiconductors that had given back ground at midweek rebounded with conviction.

The market also chose what to ignore. The Fed’s preferred inflation gauge posted its largest annual increase in three years, a reading that keeps a rate hike on the table. Equities looked straight through it — the VIX eased to 15.6. The one corner that flinched was digital assets: Bitcoin slipped to a multi-week low and the crypto-linked funds followed, the lone soft patch on an otherwise broad advance.

We were handed a hot inflation print and a fragile ceasefire, and the market closed at a record anyway. We don’t trade the headlines — we own through them. Avoid Erosion.
