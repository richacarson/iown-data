The Session

The capex faith that lifted Monday’s tape to records was tested before it could be confirmed. A Wall Street Journal report that OpenAI fell short of internal weekly-user and revenue targets in 2026 — and that the company’s CFO has privately questioned whether revenue will support future compute commitments — landed in a market that had just spent eighteen sessions bidding the semiconductor complex to fresh highs. The reaction was immediate. The S&P 500 closed at 7,139 (–0.48%), the Nasdaq fell 0.89%, and the Russell 2000 led the decline at –1.15%. NVDA reversed Monday’s record with a 1.63% loss, AMD extended Monday’s downgrade reaction with another 3.41% decline, and CRDO, which had already given back 7.45% on Monday, sold off another 8.08% to $165.92. TSM, MRVL, NXPI, and KEYS each lost more than three percent.

The selling was orderly. The VIX actually compressed 1.17% to 17.81 — this was a positioning unwind in the AI-infrastructure sleeve, not a regime change in volatility. The pattern resolves the exact thesis Monday’s brief identified: capex faith was being repriced into individual names, and the OpenAI headline gave the market the catalyst to do that repricing in size. The dispersion that opened yesterday is now a confirmed rotation, and Wednesday’s back-to-back hyperscaler prints — MSFT, GOOGL, META — will define whether the AI multiple holds or compresses further.

Energy carried the opposite tape. WTI crude crossed $100 for the first time since April 10 as the Iran proposal stalled in Washington and the United Arab Emirates announced its withdrawal from OPEC and OPEC+ effective May 1 — a structural shift that frees the cartel’s third-largest producer to expand capacity beyond its previous 3.2 million barrels per day quota. CTRA led the dividend sleeve at +2.85%, with CVX gaining 1.94%, OKE adding 2.32% ahead of tonight’s print, and VLO holding at +0.85%. The refining-margin thesis that anchors VLO and the integrated case for CVX both extended. NEE gained 1.77% on the NRC’s subsequent license renewal for both St. Lucie units, extending operations through 2056 and 2063 respectively.

Consumer Confidence printed 92.8 in April against an 89.0 consensus — the third consecutive monthly gain and the highest read of the year — supported by an improved labor-market differential. The data did not move the FOMC calculus into tomorrow’s decision. Powell is widely expected to hold and the press-conference subtext will be the May 15 Powell-Warsh transition rather than the data path.

A tape that compresses the AI sleeve while bidding the energy sleeve is communicating a healthy rotation, not a failed thesis. Monday’s dispersion has now broadened into a confirmed repricing, and the hyperscaler capex commitments Wednesday will tell us whether the AI infrastructure case stays intact or earns further compression.
