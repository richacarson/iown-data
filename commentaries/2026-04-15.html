The Session

The S&P 500 closed at 7,022 on Wednesday, recording its first all-time high since January 28 and clearing the 7,000 threshold that had served as the market’s reference point for nearly three months. The Nasdaq added 1.59% to 24,016 for its eleventh consecutive advance, led by mega-cap technology and semiconductors. The Dow slipped 0.15% as Caterpillar’s 3% decline weighed on the price-weighted average. The Russell 2000 gained 0.30%. The VIX settled at 18.17, down 1.03%, extending the volatility compression that has accompanied the rally off the late-March lows.

Today’s session was driven by a convergence of catalysts. Morgan Stanley reported a record quarter—$20.6 billion in revenue, EPS of $3.43 versus the $3.02 consensus—with equity trading revenue of $5.1 billion and advisory fees up 74% year-over-year. Bank of America beat estimates on the strength of a 21% surge in investment banking fees. The bank earnings cycle has now delivered consistently strong results across JPM, Citi, BAC, and MS, confirming that operating businesses remain robust through the war-era volatility. Separately, the SEC approved the elimination of the $25,000 pattern day-trading minimum, a regulatory shift that sent Robinhood up 10.4% and Coinbase up 6.2%.

On the geopolitical front, the White House confirmed that a second round of US-Iran negotiations is under discussion, with President Trump indicating talks could occur within days in Islamabad. Iran offered a proposal allowing ships to exit the Oman side of Hormuz free of attack, a meaningful concession that helped oil remain subdued—WTI near $91, well below the $115 March highs. The ceasefire expires April 21; the market is pricing extension rather than expiration.

The index has now recovered from the war-driven drawdown and established a new closing high. With the S&P above the January peak, the Tactical framework remains in observation mode—no trim threshold is active at current levels. The recovery validates the discipline of staying fully invested through the March correction.
