The Session

Markets opened under siege and closed in relief. The S&P 500 gained 1.16% to 6,581.76 after one of the most dramatic intraday reversals of the year, swinging from a pre-market loss of more than 1% to a broad risk-on close. The catalyst was President Trump’s announcement that the United States has engaged in diplomatic talks with Iran and will suspend all strikes against Iranian power plants and energy infrastructure for five days. The Dow rose 1.38%, the Nasdaq gained 1.38%, and the Russell 2000 led with a 2.29% advance as small caps caught the sharpest bid on de-escalation hopes.

The reversal was stark. Before the open, S&P 500 futures were trading near 6,489—roughly 1.1% below Friday’s close—after a punishing overnight session in Asia. South Korea’s Kospi had triggered circuit breakers, falling more than 6%. Japan’s Nikkei shed nearly 5%. Brent crude spiked above $114 in early trading, extending its war-era highs. The setup pointed to another leg lower. Then Trump posted that the U.S. and Iran had held “very good and productive conversations” over the prior two days, and the tone shifted instantly.

Brent collapsed more than 13% at its lows before settling around $104—still elevated, but a dramatic reversal from the morning’s trajectory. Gold continued its worst week since 1983, falling another 3.7% to $4,407 as the de-escalation narrative stripped away the geopolitical risk premium. Bitcoin rallied 4.5% to $70,884, reclaiming the $70,000 level lost over the weekend. The VIX eased to 26.15.

Iran denied that formal talks were taking place, and the five-day window is narrow. But the market’s response was unambiguous: any diplomatic opening, however tenuous, was enough to reprice the tail risk that had dominated sentiment for three weeks. Energy was the only sector to close lower as crude’s reversal unwound the war premium. Cyclicals, industrials, and semiconductors led. Caterpillar rose 3.06%. TSM gained 2.80%. The session confirmed what the portfolio has been positioned for: patience through volatility, not reaction to it.

The morning opened with circuit breakers in Seoul and Brent above $114. The close delivered the broadest risk-on session in three weeks. This is why we are positioned through events, not around them. Avoid Erosion.
