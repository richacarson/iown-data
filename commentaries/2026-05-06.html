The Session

The morning brief framed the AMD print as the validation moment for the AI infrastructure thesis. The session resolved that thesis without ambiguity. AMD opened near +18% and held to the bell at $421.47 (+18.64%) after Q1 EPS of $1.37 on $10.3B revenue and a Q2 guide of approximately $11.2B against ~$10.5B Bloomberg consensus, with Data Center revenue accelerating 57% year-over-year. The chip complex moved with it: NVDA +5.68% to $207.66, TSM +6.36%, LRCX +7.75%, KEYS +3.02%, NXPI +3.83%, QCOM +3.28%, TEL +4.46%. The S&P 500 closed at 7,366.46 (+1.48%), a fresh record. Nasdaq +2.05% to 25,846.21. Russell 2000 +1.47% — small caps participated, confirming the breadth signal Tuesday’s tape had opened.

The second narrative ran in parallel. Axios, citing two U.S. officials, reported the White House is close to a one-page Memorandum of Understanding with Iran covering enhanced UN inspections, a 12–15 year halt to enrichment, and gradual sanctions relief. President Trump confirmed progress and stated the United States will receive uranium from Iran. Crude collapsed on the framework — and the energy sleeve with it. VLO –6.61% on refining-margin compression, OKE –4.91% on midstream re-rating, CVX –3.88%, CTRA –8.62% (also pricing the Devon merger close tomorrow and the S&P 500 index exit). NTR –4.44% on the agricultural-commodity read-through.

Gold rallied 2.96% to $4,703 — counterintuitive on a peace headline, but the mechanism is structural: a Hormuz reopening drains the inflation pipeline (oil → PCE → Fed), opening the door to rate cuts in 2026. Real yields compress, the dollar weakens, and the metal extends. AEM, the IOWN gold miner, captured the move with operating leverage: +6.55% to $189.81.

The third story sat outside the macro complex. HUT ended +35.31% at $108.94 after announcing a 15-year, $9.8B triple-net lease at its Beacon Point campus in Texas — 352 MW of AI data center capacity to a high-investment-grade tenant, with renewal options that could lift contracted value to $25.1B. Total contracted backlog across two hyperscale AI campuses now stands at $16.8B. The bitcoin miner has executed the cleanest pivot to AI infrastructure in the sector. MARA carried the read-across at +7.15%.

Two open questions from the morning resolved on a single tape: AMD validated the AI cycle with hard Q2 numbers, and the Iran framework moved a step closer to credible. The setup that emerges is a market with a clearer cyclical path and a Fed with more freedom to act.
