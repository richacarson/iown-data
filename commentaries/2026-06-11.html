The Session

The market bought back what it had been selling. After three sessions of steady distribution, the S&P 500 rose +1.73% to 7,393, the Dow added +1.86%, the Nasdaq gained +2.53%, and the Russell 2000 led at +3.02% — with the recovery concentrated in precisely the names that had been sold hardest. The VIX fell 12% to 19.54. Semiconductors, the epicenter of last week’s decline, carried the tape from the open and never gave it back.

The morning’s producer price report ran hot on its face — headline PPI rose 1.1% in May against a 0.7% consensus, lifting the annual rate to 6.5% — but the composition told the same story as Wednesday’s CPI: energy is doing the work. Core PPI rose 0.4%, in line with expectations, and the market treated the print as confirmation of a known condition rather than new information. Across the Atlantic, the ECB raised its deposit rate a quarter point to 2.25%, its first hike since September 2023, citing the inflationary pressure of the war. The June 16–17 FOMC now convenes with a hot headline complex and a peer central bank already moving.

The geopolitical tape resolved in the market’s favor. The morning carried renewed escalation language out of Washington and crude whipsawed near $90; at one o’clock, Reuters reported the strikes planned for Thursday evening had been canceled, and equities took their decisive leg higher from there. The conflict premium drained out of oil, and the energy complex — Wednesday’s leader — became Thursday’s laggard.

The Growth Portfolio’s +4.72% session was the direct product of owning what the market wanted back: the semiconductor, AI-infrastructure, and digital-asset sleeves all participated with force, more than doubling IUSG’s +2.30%. The Dividend Strategy’s +1.37% rode its own semiconductor exposure and a firm defense bid, beating DVY by nearly 80 basis points. Adobe reports after tonight’s close — the next clean read on whether AI expands or displaces software demand.

The S&P 500 sits roughly 3% below its recent high — neither the trim band nor the buy tranches are engaged. Wednesday’s session and Thursday’s are the same lesson taught twice: the portfolio is built to be held through both.
