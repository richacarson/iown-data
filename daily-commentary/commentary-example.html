The Session

The tape divided cleanly along the fault line we have been watching all week. The S&P 500 closed −0.50% and the Nasdaq −1.45% as a second consecutive de-rating of the AI-hardware complex overwhelmed the broader market, while the Dow held modestly green. Beneath the index, the day was less a decline than a rotation — capital leaving semiconductors and moving into healthcare, staples and defensives.

The chip story set the tone before the open. Taiwan Semiconductor reported roughly 77% earnings growth and raised its 2026 capital-spending plan to $60–$64 billion — and still the shares gave back more than 4%. A dominant print that clears every estimate and is met with selling is the market telling you that expectations, not results, are the problem. SK Hynix fell about 11%, Korea’s KOSPI slid more than 6%, and memory names led U.S. declines.

A day like this is the barbell working exactly as intended — one sleeve leaning into growth, the other into durable cash flows, with rotation moving the weight between them without breaking the frame.

The other side of the trade belonged to us. A wave of healthcare earnings redirected the flows: Abbott jumped to the top of the S&P leaderboard on a Q2 beat, and UnitedHealth’s decisive beat carried the Dow. The Dividend Strategy gained +1.19%, edging past a flat-to-lower SPY while trailing a strong DVY. The Growth Portfolio closed −3.04% against IUSG’s −1.69%.
