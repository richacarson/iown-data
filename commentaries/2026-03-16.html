The Session

Wall Street snapped a three-week losing streak on Monday as easing Strait of Hormuz concerns and Nvidia’s GTC conference lifted risk appetite across the board. The S&P 500 rose 1.02% to 6,699.85, reclaiming ground after Friday’s close at 6,632 marked the index’s lowest level of 2026. The Dow added 0.83% to 46,946, while the Nasdaq gained 1.22% to 22,374. The Russell 2000 advanced 0.94% to 2,503. The VIX fell 13.5% to 23.51, its largest single-day decline in weeks.

The catalyst was weekend news that select tankers—including a Pakistan-bound oil vessel—successfully transited the Strait of Hormuz, suggesting a degree of Iranian leniency toward certain energy shipments. Brent crude pulled back to roughly $102 per barrel after touching $105 earlier in the session. Treasury Secretary Bessent reportedly indicated the U.S. is facilitating a coalition to escort commercial vessels through the waterway, which further eased the energy supply premium that had been building for weeks.

Semiconductors led the session with the VanEck Semiconductor ETF rising 1.7%. Nvidia gained 1.65% to $183.22 ahead of CEO Jensen Huang’s GTC keynote, where he projected $1 trillion in purchase orders for the Blackwell and Vera Rubin chip platforms through 2027—double the company’s prior estimate. Meta rose nearly 3% after reports that the company plans to lay off up to 20% of its workforce in an efficiency push tied to AI adoption. Goldman Sachs gained 2.15% and Caterpillar added 2.09%, helping the Dow outperform. Credit-sensitive sectors benefited broadly as yields retreated across the curve.

The S&P 500 remains roughly 4% below its recent high. No tactical triggers are in play. Today’s bounce was constructive—the VIX dropping back below 24 suggests the worst of the fear premium is fading—but the index would need sustained follow-through above 6,750 to suggest the correction has fully stabilized. Ownership means accepting that relief rallies and renewed pullbacks are both part of the process.
