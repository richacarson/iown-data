The Session

Two businesses we own reported before the bell this morning, and both of them raised the year. Lockheed Martin posted second-quarter sales of $20.1 billion, up 11% from a year ago, with earnings of $7.94 per share against a $7.23 consensus and free cash flow of $2.9 billion — then lifted its full-year sales outlook to roughly $79.75–$81.75 billion on what the company describes as a record $230 billion backlog. Quest Diagnostics grew revenue 10.2% to $3.04 billion, delivered adjusted earnings of $3.12 against $2.82 expected, and raised full-year guidance on both the top and bottom lines. The market paid for both: LMT +10.37%, DGX +8.60%. On a session when the S&P 500 fell 1.20% and the Nasdaq 2.15%, the Dividend Strategy closed +0.47% — ahead of DVY at −0.71% and SPY at −1.26%.

The index had its own reasons, and none of them originated inside our companies. Brent crude settled near $100.69, up about 7% in a fifth consecutive advance, after reported attacks on tankers off the Red Sea coast of Saudi Arabia. Higher energy prices carried the 10-year Treasury yield to 4.705%, its highest level since January 2025, and fed-funds futures moved to roughly an 82% probability of a September hike, up from 52% a week earlier. Weekly jobless claims of 187,000 against 212,000 expected gave the rate market nothing to work with in the other direction. Layered on top, Alphabet’s raised capital-spending guidance and Tesla’s margin compression reopened the question of when the AI build-out converts into returns — a question that was asked loudly of the businesses doing the spending, and answered quietly in favor of the businesses receiving it.

Guidance raised is the ownership event. Everything else on the tape today was weather.

That distinction ran straight through the Growth Portfolio, which closed −0.66% against IUSG at −1.63%. The contracted-compute cohort took the capital-spending news as revenue rather than risk: HUT +7.11%, CRDO +3.61% and MARA +2.90% advanced while merchant silicon gave back, with AMD −2.29% and NVDA −1.56%. Energy and power carried the rest — OKE +1.61%, VST +1.34%, CVX +0.75%. The offsets were rate-sensitive and high-beta: NOW −3.69% extended its de-rating a day after its own print, and HOOD −2.78%, SOFI −2.46% and TOL −1.63% all traded the yield move. The digital-asset sleeve eased with bitcoin at $64,847: IBIT −1.85% and ETHA −2.82%. Two sleeves, one lesson — we were paid today for owning businesses that report, not for guessing which way the barrel would settle.

Holdings News

Freeport-McMoRan delivered on Grasberg, and the tape looked past it. Second-quarter adjusted earnings of $0.74 per share on revenue of $7.03 billion and $2.0 billion of operating cash flow, with Jefferies noting EBITDA roughly 12% above consensus. Management reported the Grasberg Block Cave ramp on schedule — near 65% of capacity in the second half of 2026, approximately 80% by mid-2027 and approaching full capacity by the end of 2027. Shares eased −2.26% on the softer near-term copper outlook. We own the 2028 volumes; the July quotation is not the asset.

Old Republic wrote a more expensive quarter, and said so plainly. Net income of $322.3 million against $204.4 million a year ago, though operating income of $0.76 per share came in under the $0.79 consensus and the consolidated combined ratio widened to 95.3% from 93.6%. Net premiums and fees earned approached $2.1 billion and net investment income rose to $182.0 million. Declining to chase price in a softening underwriting market is exactly the trade-off we want this management team making. ORI closed −0.67%.
