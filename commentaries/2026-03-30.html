The Session

Monday opened on diplomatic optimism and closed on crude reality. Futures had pointed to a relief rally after weekend signals from Pakistan-hosted talks and Treasury Secretary Bessent’s pledge to restore freedom of navigation through the Strait of Hormuz. The Dow gained as much as 406 points in early trading. By the close, the S&P 500 had surrendered the entire move and then some, finishing at 6,343.75, down 0.39% for a third consecutive losing session. The Nasdaq declined 0.73% to 20,794.64. The Dow held a fractional gain of 0.11%, the only major index in the green. The Russell 2000 fell 1.46%, continuing to bear the heaviest weight of the conflict-driven rotation.

The session’s defining catalyst was oil. WTI crude settled at $102.88, its first close above $100 since July 2022. Brent finished at $112.78, capping a 55% monthly gain—the largest for the contract since its inception in 1988, surpassing the previous record set during the Gulf War. The energy shock is now firmly a macro event: gas prices approaching $4 per gallon nationally, airline surcharges rising, and industrial input costs climbing across sectors.

Federal Reserve Chair Powell spoke at Harvard’s introductory economics class in what may be one of his final public appearances before his term ends in May. The message was measured: rates are in a “good place,” the Fed will look through the oil shock rather than hike into it, and inflation expectations remain anchored. Market-implied odds of a rate increase this year fell from above 50% on Friday to roughly 2% after the remarks. The removal of near-term tightening risk was constructive, but it was not enough to overcome the session’s headwinds.

Technology led the decline. Semiconductors extended their pullback as Micron fell 10% in late trading on continued fallout from Google’s TurboQuant algorithm announcement, which has raised questions about future memory demand intensity. LRCX fell 5.43%, TSM declined 3.13%, and AMD shed 2.95%. The broader tech selloff reflects a regime shift from AI optimism to geopolitical repricing that has defined March.

The pattern the morning brief identified—hope trades bought and sold within 48 hours—played out in a single session. The portfolio does not trade the pattern. It is positioned through it.
