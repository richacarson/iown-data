The Session

The S&P 500 rose 1.17% to 6,967, pressing within reach of the 7,000 level for the first time since the Iran conflict began in late February. The Nasdaq added 1.96% for its tenth consecutive advancing session, its longest such streak since 2021. The Dow gained 0.66%. The Russell 2000 rose 1.32%. The VIX declined 3.87% to 18.38, continuing the steady compression of implied volatility that has characterized this rally.

The session absorbed two substantial macro inputs and digested them constructively. March PPI came in at +0.5% month-over-month, well below the +1.1% consensus estimate. Core PPI rose just 0.1% against expectations for 0.5%. The headline number confirmed that energy is driving the pipeline inflation—gasoline surged 15.7%, diesel 42%—but services costs were flat and core pressures remain contained. The market read it as a favorable pass-through story: the energy shock is real but narrower than feared.

Separately, the IMF published its World Economic Outlook, cutting 2026 global growth to 3.1% from 3.3% in January. The fund warned that the world is drifting toward its adverse scenario, in which oil prices persist near $100 and growth drops to 2.5%. U.S. growth was trimmed only marginally to 2.3%, reflecting the net energy exporter advantage. The market chose the PPI print over the IMF warning, continuing to price a short-lived conflict and a second-half normalization.

Bank earnings were mixed. JPMorgan beat but lowered net interest income guidance and slipped 1%. Wells Fargo missed on revenue and credit quality, falling 5%. BlackRock climbed 4% on strong results. Johnson & Johnson beat estimates and raised full-year guidance. The early Q1 reads suggest corporate earnings remain resilient even as the macro backdrop has shifted.

The morning brief flagged Carson’s major resistance at SPY $691.90–$693.20 as Tuesday’s focal point. SPY closed above that zone, resolving the test to the upside. The S&P’s push toward 7,000 now faces clear air above the pre-war technical structure. Positioning remains the tailwind—underweight accounts are chasing this rally. Stay the course.
