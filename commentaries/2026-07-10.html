The Session

The broad tape closed higher, but the leadership behind it narrowed sharply. The S&P 500 rose 0.41% to 7,574.71, the Dow added 0.29% and the Nasdaq 0.29%, while the Russell 2000 slipped 0.49% — breadth quietly softened even as the headline indices advanced. The VIX eased to 15.03.

Friday’s marquee event was SK Hynix’s Nasdaq debut. The South Korean memory maker and Nvidia supplier opened near $170, roughly 14% above its offering price, and raised about $26.5 billion — reported as the largest U.S. listing ever by a foreign company. The debut anchored an AI-memory trade that pulled the day’s bid toward the megacaps: Nvidia rose 4.03% to $210.96, clearing $210 for the first time this week, and AMD added 2.04%.

That concentration came at the expense of Thursday’s broader chip rally. The equipment and connectivity names that led the prior session consolidated — Marvell eased 3.07%, Credo 2.96%, Fortinet 3.80% and Lam Research 0.80% — and the crypto miners handed back their outsized Thursday gains, with MARA off 4.69% and Hut 8 down 3.77%. The give-back in these peripheral names is what left the Growth Portfolio modestly lower on a green market day.

Underneath, a steady value bid did the quieter work. Steel Dynamics rose 2.86%, Caterpillar 1.49%, Colgate 1.35% and NextEra 0.99% as easing Treasury yields and softer oil supported the rate-sensitive and defensive corners that populate the Dividend sleeve. Oil slipped as President Trump said the U.S.-Iran ceasefire was over but talks would continue; Bitcoin firmed about 1% to roughly $63,800, and the digital-asset ETFs followed, with ETHA up 2.58% and IBIT 1.17%.

Narrowing leadership is a familiar feature of a momentum tape, not a reason to chase the day’s winners or abandon the day’s laggards. We finished this week’s rebalance into businesses we intend to own through exactly this kind of rotation — the framework stays in own-and-hold mode, with no drawdown trigger in view.
