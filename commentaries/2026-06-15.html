The Session

A framework does to a war premium exactly what you would expect. Word over the weekend that the U.S.–Iran agreement is complete — signed as a memorandum of understanding Sunday, with a formal ceremony set for Friday in Switzerland — left Monday’s tape to spend the day pricing out the geopolitical risk that had anchored crude since late February. The S&P 500 closed at 7,555.26, higher by 1.67%; the Nasdaq added 3.08% and the Dow 0.92%. Leadership ran to the parts of the market most levered to lower oil and lower rates, and pressure fell on the parts most levered to the premium that just began to drain away.

The mechanics were straightforward. U.S. crude fell roughly 5% toward $80 a barrel and Brent slipped below $83, both back to levels last seen in early March, as the agreement cleared a path for the Strait of Hormuz to reopen and the naval blockade to lift. Even after the move, crude remains well above where it started the year — the de-escalation removes a premium rather than the underlying market. Energy was the day’s clear laggard across the complex: refiners, producers, and midstream names all gave back ground as the supply-disruption trade unwound.

The other side of the ledger did the lifting. The volatility complex compressed hard — the VIX fell 9.22% to 16.05 as fear pricing came out of the market — while gold gained 2.43% to $4,341.80 and bitcoin added 4.32% to $66,561. Semiconductors and the broader AI-infrastructure cohort led the advance, and the digital-asset sleeve moved in step with a risk-on bid that reached across equities, metals, and crypto at once.

For the committee, the session split the two books along a single seam. The Growth Portfolio, with its AI-infrastructure backbone and digital-asset exposure, finished higher by 2.68%. The Dividend Strategy, carrying the energy weight that bore the brunt of the crude move, held to –0.37% — a far steadier result than the –1.63% turned in by its dividend benchmark, DVY, which is more heavily concentrated in the value and energy names that led the tape lower.

A single agreement can lift the growth book and press the dividend book in the same session. We do not trade the headline — we own the businesses on both sides of it.

Holdings News

AMD drew two upgrades into the open. Citi’s Atif Malik moved the stock to Buy from Neutral and lifted his target to $575 from $460, while Bank of America raised its target to $560. Both notes center on the previously disclosed six-gigawatt, four-year Meta partnership built around custom MI450 chips, with Citi’s model translating each gigawatt of capacity into roughly $15 billion of revenue. The next company catalyst is AMD’s Advancing AI 2026 event on July 22–23.

Qualcomm heads toward its June 24 Investor Day. The event frames the near-term setup for the name, with the Street focused on the company’s diversification beyond handsets into automotive and edge-AI silicon.

The energy complex repriced as a group. The session’s pressure on VLO, DVN, CVX, OKE and CNX was a single macro re-rating tied to the Brent premium draining out of crude — not a verdict on individual cash flows. The energy sleeve’s shareholder-return frameworks are unchanged under a lower-volatility oil regime.
