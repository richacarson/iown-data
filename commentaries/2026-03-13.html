The Session

The S&P 500 fell 0.61% to 6,631.88 — its fourth straight decline and a new 2026 closing low. The index posted a 1.6% weekly loss and its first three-week losing streak in about a year. The Dow held up better, dipping just 0.26%, while the Nasdaq dropped 0.93% as software and tech names led the downside. The VIX was essentially flat at 27.19.

The session opened higher after oil pulled back modestly and PCE data came in stable — core PCE at +0.4% MoM for January, pre-war. But gains faded through the afternoon as Q4 GDP was revised down to 2.1% from the initial estimate, and Brent crude settled at $103.14. Adobe fell 6% after its CEO announced his departure. Consumer sentiment dipped to 55.5 as expectations fell 4.4%. Gold dropped 2.04% to $5,021. Bitcoin rose 1.16% to $71,347, continuing its decoupling from equities.

The market enters a pivotal week. NVIDIA’s GTC conference Monday is the biggest AI catalyst since Broadcom’s earnings. The FOMC meets Wednesday — expected to hold, but Powell’s press conference will set tone for the rest of Q1. Iran let two gas tankers through Hormuz today, a small signal, but the broader conflict shows no sign of resolution.
