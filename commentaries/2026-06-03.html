The Session

One session after closing at a record 7,609.78, the broad tape eased back across the board — the S&P 500 –0.72%, the Dow –1.21%, the Nasdaq –0.85%, and the Russell 2000 –1.31%. The proximate catalyst was, paradoxically, good news. ADP private payrolls rose 122,000 in May, the strongest reading since January 2025 and above the roughly 110,000 consensus, with hiring more broad-based than it has been in years. A labor market carrying that kind of momentum narrows the case for near-term Fed easing into the June 16–17 meeting, and the market chose to read strength as a reason to fade rate-cut expectations rather than to celebrate growth. The VIX firmed 1.8% to 16.05 and gold gave back 1.0%.

Beneath the index level, the AI-infrastructure complex split rather than broke. The custom-silicon, analog, and equipment cohort closed green — AMD ↑4.13%, Marvell ↑3.73% (extending Tuesday’s record session past $300 intraday), Qualcomm ↑3.89%, Analog Devices ↑3.42%, and Lam Research ↑2.78% — while the merchant-GPU and foundry names gave back, with Nvidia –3.62%, Taiwan Semiconductor –2.24%, and Credo –6.29%. The rotation lined up with the after-close calendar: Broadcom reports fiscal Q2 results this evening, a print analysts have framed as the next real test for the chip rally, and capital appeared to pre-position toward the custom-ASIC side of the trade.

Oil reasserted itself for a third consecutive session. Brent climbed toward $97 a barrel as Hezbollah rockets tested the U.S.-mediated truce and President Trump and Prime Minister Netanyahu traded public barbs, keeping the war-risk premium intact even as a possible Strait of Hormuz memorandum was floated. The energy sleeve caught the bid — Valero ↑1.24%, Chevron ↑1.21%, ONEOK steady. The net of it: the income sleeve’s industrial and semiconductor-cyclical tilt — Qualcomm, Analog Devices, Fastenal ↑3.87%, Caterpillar ↑1.80%, Lam Research — outweighed staples softness in Colgate and Church & Dwight to finish the day green, while the Growth Portfolio’s crypto-adjacent and mega-cap-GPU exposure left it fractionally lower.

The S&P sits comfortably above its 200-day average and within record territory; a –0.72% step back from an all-time high does not approach any IOWN buy tranche, which begin at a 20% drawdown from the peak. This is consolidation following nine straight up weeks, not a signal. We own through it.
