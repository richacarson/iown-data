Credit Cracks Widen
Private credit stress spreads. Oil resumes climb. S&P –1.53%. Morgan Stanley caps withdrawals. FOMC next Wednesday.
The Session

Private credit stress and resurgent oil drove the S&P 500 down 1.53% to 6,671.81. The Dow fell 1.56%, the Nasdaq dropped 1.78%, and the Russell 2000 declined 2.12%. The VIX rose 12.42% to 27.24. All eleven S&P sectors finished red.

The financial sector was at the center of the selling. Morgan Stanley and Cliffwater joined BlackRock in capping fund withdrawals from private credit vehicles. JPMorgan restricted lending to private credit providers after marking down several loans. Goldman Sachs fell 3.7%, Morgan Stanley 4%. The concern is contagion—if redemption pressure forces fire sales of illiquid private loans, it could ripple into broader credit markets.

Oil resumed its climb as strikes between Iran, Israel, and GCC members intensified. Tankers continued to avoid the Strait of Hormuz. The IEA announced a release of 400 million barrels from strategic reserves, but it failed to contain the rally. Gold fell 1.65% to $5,094 as the dollar strengthened. Bitcoin held steady, up 0.25%—decoupling from the equity sell-off. February PPI arrives March 18 alongside the FOMC decision.

IOWN Tactical: S&P at ~6,672. Approaching our monitoring range but still above the –20% buy trigger (~5,800 from ATH). The dual pressure of energy inflation and financial-sector stress is the market’s current challenge. Ownership discipline matters most in weeks like this.
