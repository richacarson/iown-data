The Session

The morning brief framed Wednesday’s hawkish dot plot as a growth-cost trade rather than a clean inflation fight, and Thursday’s tape resolved squarely in that direction. Equities recovered the ground lost in the post-Fed selloff, with the S&P 500 closing +1.05% at 7,497.86, the Nasdaq +1.91%, and the Russell 2000 +2.12%. The VIX fell 10.47% to 16.51, unwinding the vol bid that built into the FOMC. This was a clean risk-on session, and growth led it.

Two macro resolutions drove the rotation. First, the interim U.S.-Iran agreement took effect: Vice President Vance confirmed the 60-day period began Thursday, and early signs emerged of vessels resuming transit through the Strait of Hormuz. Oil cracked toward multi-month lows — WTI fell below $75 and Brent toward $78, the lowest levels since early March. That relief priced directly out of the trades that had worked during the conflict. Energy gave back (CVX –2.22%, VLO –1.45%, DVN –1.08%, OKE –0.64%), defense rotated lower as the deal reduced near-term urgency (LMT –4.01%, GD –3.53%), and the haven complex retreated (Gold –3.33%, Bitcoin –2.09%).

Second, semiconductors led the recovery. Intel’s announced U.S. chip partnership with Apple set the early tone, and AI-demand momentum carried the cohort through the close. Credo rose 9.02%, Marvell 7.27%, TSMC 6.94%, Qualcomm 6.17%, NXP 5.05%, AMD 4.86%, and Analog Devices 4.83%. The chip strength spanned both sleeves, but the Growth Portfolio captured it more cleanly: +2.42% on the day against IUSG’s +1.71%.

The Dividend Strategy tells the mirror-image story. At –0.44% it trailed both DVY (–0.26%) and the S&P (+1.04%), even though its own chip names — Analog Devices, Qualcomm, Lam Research (+3.97%) — participated fully. The drag came from the sleeve’s ballast: defense (LMT, GD), steel (STLD –7.49%, pulling back from near 52-week highs as the materials trade unwound), materials (NTR –2.21%), and staples. The same defensive composition that cushioned the sleeve on Wednesday’s down day became the headwind on Thursday’s up day. That is the symmetry, not a surprise.

A risk-on session that reverses the war trade is not an invitation to chase it. Our discipline sits at the index level — with the S&P at 7,497.86, today’s tape moved neither a trim trigger nor a tranche-buy level. We own the businesses through the rotation.
