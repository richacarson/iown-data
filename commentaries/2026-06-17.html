The Session

Kevin Warsh kept the fed funds rate unchanged at 3.50–3.75% in his first meeting as Chair, exactly as priced—and then let the projections do the talking. The new dot plot no longer carries a 2026 cut; the median year-end rate moved up to 3.8% from 3.4% in March, with nine of eighteen officials now penciling in at least one hike this year. The two-year Treasury yield jumped roughly fifteen basis points to its highest in over a year. Equities, which had carried the Dow to a fresh intraday record earlier in the session, faded into the close: the S&P 500 finished –1.19%, the Nasdaq –1.34%.

The morning’s open question was whether Warsh would simply drop the last projected cut. The answer went a step further—the committee shifted to a hike-biased posture and removed the easing tilt, and Warsh himself declined to submit a dot. His press conference was deliberately spare; pressed for elaboration, he noted he had nothing more to add than the statement itself, while reaffirming what he called a unanimous commitment to price stability.

The internals rewarded the rotation we have been tracking. Rate-sensitive housing and the crypto complex gave ground, while the semiconductor cohort that mean-reverted on Tuesday rebounded—MRVL, CRDO, AMD, TSM, and LRCX all closed green. That counter-trend bid in chips let the Growth Portfolio finish essentially flat against an IUSG that fell 1.12%. The Dividend Strategy gave back about a point but still outpaced both DVY and SPY on the day, its defensive-cyclical anchors—CAT among them—catching the same bid that briefly lifted the Dow to a record before the late reversal. A higher path for rates reprices valuations and the cost of carry; it does not rewrite the cash-flow durability of the businesses we own.

A hawkish dot plot reprices the path, not the enterprises behind our holdings. Carson’s SPY $645.80 support sits well below today’s $741 close—a downside reference, not an actionable marker, and far from the drawdown tranches that would trigger deployment. We let the framework, not the forecast, dictate action.
