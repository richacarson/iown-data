The Session

The S&P 500 finished at 6,617, up 0.08%, after trimming a 1.2% intraday loss on reports that Pakistan requested a two-week extension of Trump’s 8 PM deadline for Iran. The reversal was sharp and instructive. Through midday, equities were under broad pressure as the President escalated rhetoric on Truth Social, warning that Iranian civilian infrastructure could face strikes by midnight. Tech bore the brunt of the risk-off move: Apple fell nearly 4% on foldable iPhone development setbacks, while Nvidia, AMD, and Micron each declined roughly 1%. Tesla dropped over 2%. The Dow lost 85 points (–0.18%) and the Nasdaq eked out a 0.10% gain.

The session’s character was defined by two competing narratives: escalation risk from the approaching deadline, and incremental diplomatic signals suggesting the ultimatum may again extend. Pakistan’s foreign minister formally requested a two-week delay while urging Tehran to begin reopening the Strait. Iran’s position remained unchanged—permanent cessation of hostilities or the waterway stays closed—but the market read the Pakistani intervention as evidence the deadline might pass without the threatened infrastructure campaign. The S&P trimmed its losses from –1.2% at the lows to nearly flat by the close, with energy and defensive names leading the recovery.

Crude oil settled near $113 (WTI) after touching $116 intraday. Gold rose 1.0% to $4,732, reasserting its safe-haven bid. The 10-year yield held at 4.34%, consistent with zero rate cuts priced for 2026. The VIX surged 6.7% to 25.78, reflecting the options market’s demand for protection into the overnight window. Bitcoin declined 0.9% to $69,360, continuing to trade sideways through the conflict.

UBS lowered its year-end S&P 500 target to 7,500 from 7,700, citing energy-driven inflation and supply-side constraints that the Fed cannot address with rate policy. Goldman Sachs, meanwhile, noted that the technology sector’s valuation premium relative to expected growth has fallen to its lowest level since the 1970s—a setup they characterized as a potential opportunity once geopolitical risk subsides.

The morning brief framed tonight’s deadline as a binary event. The session confirmed it: equities fell on escalation rhetoric and recovered on diplomatic signals, all within the same trading day. The portfolio’s construction—energy exposure, defense holdings, dividend cash flows—performed as designed through the compression.
