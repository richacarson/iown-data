The Session

The tape closed the week back at fresh records. The S&P 500 finished at 7,397.43 (+0.82%), the Nasdaq added 1.71% to 26,247.08, and the Dow held flat at 49,609.15. The Russell 2000 added 0.76% to 2,861.21. VIX edged up to 17.19 even as the index complex pushed higher, the muted volatility footprint suggesting positioning rather than panic on the backdrop of a continuing Hormuz exchange.

The session resolved Thursday’s open question cleanly. April payrolls printed 115,000 against the 62,000 consensus and unemployment held at 4.3%, with average hourly earnings at +0.2% month-over-month coming in slightly below the +0.3% estimate. The combination — firmer hiring without an accompanying wage acceleration — is the configuration the equity tape rewards: economic resilience without the pressure that would force a hawkish Fed pivot. Markets shrugged off the Michigan preliminary sentiment print at 48.2 (versus 49.5 expected, a fresh record low) because the inflation expectation series within the survey actually softened — one-year to 4.5% from 4.7%, five-year to 3.4% from 3.5%.

Beneath the index print, the AI semiconductor complex carried the weight. AMD added 11.44% to $455.19 extending Wednesday’s post-earnings move; QCOM advanced 8.17% to $219.09 on a Daiwa upgrade to Outperform with the price target raised to $225 from $140; FTNT continued its post-print rally with another 5.65% gain to $114.07. The cybersecurity, semicap, and data-center silicon sleeves all repriced higher in concert. Healthcare remained the consistent laggard — ABT (–3.09%) and SYK (–2.98%) anchored the dividend book’s decliners as the sector continued to trade through its own multi-month drawdown.

Records on a strong jobs print with vol contained and the AI complex leading is the textbook risk-on resolution. The committee’s framework remains unchanged — with the S&P 500 well above 200DMA and now at fresh highs, the BUY tranches sit dormant. The tactical posture is patience.
